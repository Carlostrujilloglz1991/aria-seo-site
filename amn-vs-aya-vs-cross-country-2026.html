AMN vs Aya vs Cross Country: Travel Agency Head-to-Head

Part of the Travel Nurse Hub — agencies, contracts, stipends, and taxes in one map.

Three names dominate travel nursing: AMN Healthcare, Aya Healthcare, and Cross Country Nurses. All three are large, legitimate, and place thousands of nurses — but they don't feel the same to work with. Pay transparency, job volume, how the app works, and how your recruiter treats you differ enough to shape your whole assignment. Here's an honest, side-by-side comparison to help you choose.

The short version: Aya is prized for a huge, transparent, searchable job board and a slick app — great for nurses who like to self-serve and see real pay upfront. AMN is one of the largest and most established agencies with deep hospital relationships (many exclusive/MSP contracts), broad benefits, and a more traditional recruiter model. Cross Country is a long-established agency known for a personal, relationship-driven recruiter experience. Most serious travelers sign with more than one agency anyway.

Why you shouldn't pick just one

Before comparing, understand the most important tactic in travel nursing: work with multiple agencies at once. No single agency has every job, and pay packages for the same hospital contract can differ between agencies. Signing with two or three lets you compare live offers and gives you leverage. Think of this comparison as "which two or three should anchor your search," not "which one wins." For the fundamentals, see the best travel nursing agencies guide.

Aya Healthcare — volume and transparency

Aya is one of the largest travel-nursing marketplaces, and its calling card is a big, well-organized job board where you can see pay packages and search openings yourself. Nurses who like to compare listings without waiting on a recruiter tend to love it. Because Aya also operates as a vendor management/MSP in many systems, it often has strong visibility into available contracts. If you value transparency and a modern app, Aya usually anchors the search.

AMN Healthcare — scale and hospital relationships

AMN is one of the oldest and largest healthcare staffing companies in the country. Its strength is depth of hospital relationships — as an MSP for many health systems, AMN sometimes holds jobs other agencies can't access. It offers broad benefits and a full-service, recruiter-guided model. Travelers who want an established name with wide reach and are comfortable working closely with a recruiter often keep AMN in their rotation.

Cross Country Nurses — the relationship model

Cross Country is a long-established agency whose reputation leans on recruiter relationships and personal service. Nurses who prefer a recruiter who knows them, advocates on pay, and helps navigate credentialing and housing often report a good experience here. If you're newer to travel and want more hand-holding, Cross Country is worth a conversation.

How to actually compare offers

Compare the whole package, not the "blended rate." Travel pay is a bundle: taxable hourly base + non-taxable stipends (housing, meals/incidentals) + any travel/completion bonuses. Two agencies can quote similar totals with very different splits. Always ask for the breakdown, confirm the stipend amounts, the guaranteed hours, cancellation/float policy, and start date. A higher headline number with fewer guaranteed hours can pay less in reality.

Also watch the tax side: those non-taxable stipends only stay non-taxable if you maintain a legitimate tax home. That's a whole topic on its own — see travel nurse tax home before you assume the stipend is free money.

Red flags that apply to any agency

Watch for: vague pay packages that won't break down taxable vs stipend; pressure to accept before you've seen the contract in writing; low or no guaranteed hours (you can get floated or canceled); and stipends set suspiciously high relative to the base (an IRS audit risk if they don't reflect real housing costs). A good recruiter answers all of these plainly. If yours won't, that's your answer.

Bottom line

All three are legitimate, large agencies — the "best" one is the one whose model fits how you like to work. Reach for Aya if you want transparency and a strong self-service job board, AMN for scale and access to exclusive hospital contracts, and Cross Country if you want a hands-on recruiter relationship. Then do what experienced travelers do: sign with two or three, compare live packages for the same jobs, and let them compete for you. That competition — not brand loyalty — is what maximizes your pay.
