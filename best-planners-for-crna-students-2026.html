Best Planners for CRNA Students (SRNAs) in 2026

Last updated: June 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes | By a CRNA applicant turned SRNA

CRNA school is a logistical and cognitive marathon. The students who finish without burning out — and who actually learn the material rather than just surviving it — are the organized ones. Not because they're naturally more organized, but because they built systems at the start. A good planner is a foundation of that system.

Quick picks: Academic planner → Graduate Student Weekly Planner. Case tracking → SRNA Anesthesia Case Log. Board prep → NBCRNA SEE/NCE Exam Prep Journal.

1. Graduate-Level Academic Planner

The traditional academic planner doesn't work for CRNA school. You're managing clinical rotations, didactic exams, simulation lab competencies, case minimum requirements, and reading assignments simultaneously — and rarely on a standard Monday-Friday schedule.

What SRNAs need in a planner: weekly blocks that accommodate 12-hour clinical days, clinical site and rotation tracking, exam schedule integration, case number running totals, reading assignment tracking by course, and space for daily clinical notes. Search Amazon for weekly academic planners.

2. SRNA Anesthesia Case Log Book

CRNA programs require documented case logs for accreditation compliance and your own professional record. Most programs have digital systems, but a paper log serves as backup and personal reference. Track: case number, date, site, attending CRNA/physician, patient ASA class, procedure type, anesthetic technique, blocks performed, airway management details, and clinical pearls.

After graduation, your case log documents the breadth of your training — valuable for employment credentialing. View our SRNA Anesthesia Case Log.

3. Pharmacology Quick Reference Journal

CRNA pharmacology is high volume and high stakes. Building your own reference journal during the didactic phase — organized by drug class, with mechanism, dosing, onset/peak/duration, and clinical pearls you write yourself — beats any printed study guide because the act of writing it reinforces learning. Search Amazon for blank notebooks suitable for this purpose.

4. Board Exam Prep Tracker (SEE/NCE)

The NBCRNA Certification Examination (NCE) and Standardized Entry Examination (SEE) require systematic preparation. A board exam prep journal tracks: content domain coverage, question bank performance by category, weak area identification, review session logs, and score trend tracking across practice exams. Starting this journal in year one — not just before the exam — builds a comprehensive review document when you need it. Search Amazon.

5. Clinical Reflection Journal

Anesthesia is learned at the bedside. The cases that teach you the most — the difficult airways, the hemodynamic instability, the unexpected complications — need to be processed intentionally to extract their full learning value. A clinical reflection journal captures: what happened, what you anticipated vs. what occurred, what the attending did that you want to remember, and what you'd do differently. This builds the clinical wisdom that transforms competent providers into excellent ones. Search Amazon.
