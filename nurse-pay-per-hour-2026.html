Nurse Pay Per Hour 2026

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

Annual salary numbers obscure what nurses actually earn per hour worked — and the differential between base rate, night shift, weekend, and overtime makes the real number considerably more useful for comparison.

The base vs. effective rate gap: A nurse earning $42/hr base who works every other weekend with a $6/hr weekend differential and two night shifts per week with a $5/hr night differential effectively earns $47–$48/hr average — 12–14% above base. Over a year of 36-hour weeks, that differential adds $6,000–$8,000 to gross income invisibly.

How Overtime Changes the Math

A nurse at $48/hr base who picks up one extra 12-hour shift per week earns $72/hr for those hours (1.5x overtime). That single extra shift generates $864 gross per week — $3,456/month, $41,472/year — on top of their base salary. The nurses clearing $130,000+ as staff RNs are almost always doing this: a base salary plus consistent overtime or per diem at overtime rates.

Fatigue and safety considerations are real. A nurse working 60+ hours per week consistently is running a risk to both their health and patient safety that the income doesn't fully compensate for. The sustainable overtime play is selective — a few extra shifts per month during strong staffing periods, not every week indefinitely.

Travel Nurse Hourly Equivalent

Travel nursing packages are quoted as weekly totals ($3,200/week) that blend taxable hourly pay with non-taxable stipends. The taxable base is often set artificially low ($18–$22/hr) while stipends make up the rest. The true hourly equivalent — dividing total weekly package by 36 hours — is $88/hr for a $3,200 package. That's the fair comparison to a $58/hr staff ICU rate: the travel nurse is effectively earning 52% more per hour on the same shift, with a defined 13-week commitment and no long-term strings attached.

The number to know before negotiating: Your effective hourly rate including all differentials and your average hours per pay period. If you work 36 hrs/week base but average 40 hrs/week including OT and charge shifts, your effective rate is higher than your posted hourly. Know this number before a recruiter quotes you a travel package — it's the only honest comparison.
