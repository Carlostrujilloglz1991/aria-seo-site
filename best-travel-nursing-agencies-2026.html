1. Travel Nurse Across America (TNAA)

BEST FOR: Experienced ICU / ER nurses who negotiate

TNAA has earned a near-cult following among experienced travel nurses — and for good reason. They are known for having recruiters who actually answer the phone, pay packages that hold up under scrutiny, and a culture that feels less like a staffing mill. Their specialty depth in critical care, OR, and emergency is particularly strong.

TNAA also provides 24/7 on-call clinical support — a big deal if you are at a new facility at 2 AM and need backup. Their health insurance is day-one eligible, which not every agency offers.

Pros:
  • Consistently rated #1 by travelers for recruiter quality
  • Day-one health insurance
  • 24/7 clinical support line
  • Strong in crisis and high-demand markets
  • Transparent pay breakdown on request
Cons:
  • Smaller job board than Aya or AMN
  • Less coverage in some rural markets
  • Recruiter quality can vary by region

2. Host Healthcare

BEST FOR: First-time travel nurses

Host Healthcare has developed a reputation for treating their travelers like adults — they will explain pay packages, walk you through your first compliance checklist, and assign recruiters who have often traveled themselves. If you are new to travel nursing, Host is the agency most likely to guide you without being condescending.

Their weekly pay is competitive, their job board is solid, and they have a particularly strong presence in California, Texas, and Florida.

Pros:
  • Excellent onboarding support for first-timers
  • Recruiters known for responsiveness
  • Good pay in high-demand markets
  • Strong in coastal states
  • $600 travel reimbursement per assignment
Cons:
  • Job volume smaller than Aya or AMN
  • Benefits enrollment window can be short

3. Aya Healthcare

BEST FOR: Nurses who want maximum assignment options

Aya is the largest travel nursing agency in the country by job volume. If you want to compare hundreds of assignments in one place, Aya's platform is hard to beat. Their self-service portal lets you browse openings without talking to a recruiter first, which many nurses prefer. The trade-off: with scale comes variability. Get everything in writing.

Pros:
  • Largest job volume of any agency
  • Excellent self-service job search portal
  • Covers almost every state and specialty
  • Competitive pay in high-demand markets
Cons:
  • Recruiter quality varies widely
  • Can feel impersonal at scale
  • Slower issue resolution reported by some nurses

4. AMN Healthcare

BEST FOR: Nurses who prioritize benefits and stability

AMN is the oldest and largest healthcare staffing company in the country. If comprehensive benefits are your top priority — dental, vision, 401k matching, tuition reimbursement — AMN is worth the trade-off in pay rates, which typically run slightly below smaller boutique agencies. They also offer proprietary housing through AMN Housing.

Pros:
  • Industry-leading benefits package
  • Proprietary housing option available
  • Huge hospital network with exclusive contracts
  • Established track record and financial stability
Cons:
  • Pay typically lower than boutique agencies
  • Larger company means less personal recruiter experience
  • Less flexibility in contract negotiation

Trustaff is a mid-size agency with strong relationships in Ohio, Tennessee, and the broader Southeast and Midwest. If you are targeting those regions, they often have access to assignments the national giants do not. Recruiter responsiveness is generally well-rated.

Pros:
  • Strong regional presence in Midwest and Southeast

6. Stability Healthcare

BEST FOR: High-pay seekers who will negotiate hard

Stability Healthcare is a boutique agency that punches above its weight on pay — particularly for critical care, L&D, and OR nurses. They operate with a model that aims to give travelers more of the bill rate. Less hand-holding for new travelers, but for experienced nurses who know what they want, Stability can deliver top-tier compensation.

Pros:
  • Among highest pay rates in the industry
  • Good for experienced negotiators
  • Strong in critical care and OR specialties
Cons:
  • Smaller job volume
  • Less support infrastructure for new travelers

7. Fusion Medical Staffing

BEST FOR: Allied health professionals and RNs wanting one agency for both

Fusion Medical Staffing places both nurses and allied health professionals (PT, OT, radiology, respiratory). If you or your travel partner work in allied health, Fusion can coordinate placements for both of you. Their nursing placement is solid, though specialty depth is not as strong in critical care as TNAA or Stability.

Pros:
  • Covers nursing and allied health
  • Good for couples traveling together in different roles
  • Warm, responsive company culture
Cons:
  • Not the strongest for critical care specialists
  • Pay is competitive but rarely tops the market

How to Pick the Right Agency for You

The single most important thing experienced travelers will tell you: work with 2-3 agencies simultaneously. This is not disloyal — it is strategic. Recruiters know it happens. Competition between your agencies means better offers, faster responses, and more options.

If you are a first-time traveler: Start with Host Healthcare and TNAA. Both are known for recruiter quality and will help you navigate your first contract without overselling you on a miserable assignment.

If pay is your top priority: Add Stability Healthcare or TNAA to your mix and negotiate explicitly. Ask your recruiter to show you the full pay package breakdown including taxable base, non-taxable stipends, and bill rate. A recruiter who will not show you the breakdown is a red flag.

If you want maximum location flexibility: Aya Healthcare's job board plus a boutique agency like TNAA gives you the best of both worlds — volume and quality.

If benefits matter more than peak pay: AMN Healthcare is your best option for health, dental, vision, and retirement coverage.

Start your applications now — most agencies take 1-2 weeks to fully onboard you, and the best assignments go fast.
