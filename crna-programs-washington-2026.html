CRNA Programs in Washington 2026

Washington has exactly one accredited nurse anesthesia program — Gonzaga University in Spokane — and it's growing. For Washington ICU nurses that means a single in-state target plus a short list of strong out-of-state options. Here's how to build a competitive plan around it.

Washington CRNA market context: Gonzaga University's Doctor of Nurse Anesthesia Practice (DNAP) in Spokane is the state's only COA-accredited program. It runs about 36 months with over 2,800 hours of supervised clinical experience across Washington, Idaho, Montana, and Oregon, and is expanding its annual class from 28 to 40 students — moving into the UW School of Medicine–Gonzaga building in Spokane. With one in-state program, most Washington applicants also apply across the border to Oregon.

Gonzaga University DNAP

Gonzaga's program is operated in partnership with Providence Sacred Heart Medical Center in Spokane and emphasizes leadership, systems thinking, and evidence-based practice. The 36-month curriculum integrates more than 2,800 hours of supervised clinical experience across regional sites in Washington, Idaho, Montana, and Oregon, preparing graduates for the National Certification Examination. The program has historically drawn a high volume of competitive applicants for a small cohort.

Two changes make 2026–2027 a notable window. First, Gonzaga is expanding each entering class from roughly 28 to 40 students — meaningfully more seats in a state that has only one program. Second, the program is moving into newly built space in the UW School of Medicine–Gonzaga partnership building in Spokane. Applicants should verify the current application cycle directly, as recent cycles have opened in mid-January with classes starting in May.

The One-Program Reality and Your Backup List

With a single in-state program, Washington applicants should plan to apply broadly. Oregon is the natural extension — Oregon Health & Science University in Portland is a well-regarded option within driving distance. Many Washington nurses also look to Gonzaga's regional clinical footprint in Idaho and Montana. Treat Gonzaga as your anchor, then add two or three out-of-state programs so your odds aren't tied to one small cohort, even with the expansion to 40 seats.

Don't apply only to Gonzaga: Even with the class growing to 40, demand for Washington's single program is high. Pair Gonzaga with OHSU in Oregon and one or two other regional programs. Relocating for school is common for Washington applicants — budget for it.
Washington CRNA salary on graduation: New CRNAs in Washington commonly earn well into the low-to-mid $200,000s, with Seattle's high surgical volume and rural eastern-Washington demand both supporting strong pay.
