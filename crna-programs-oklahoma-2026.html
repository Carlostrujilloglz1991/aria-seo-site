CRNA Programs in Oklahoma

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

Oklahoma now has three accredited nurse anesthesia programs: the long-established Oklahoma City University DNAP, the University of Tulsa DNP, and a brand-new public pathway at the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center, approved in 2025. For in-state applicants, that's a wider pipeline than most states offer.

The landscape: Oklahoma now has three COA-accredited nurse anesthesia programs — Oklahoma City University (DNAP, long-established), the University of Tulsa (DNP), and the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center (DNP), approved in 2025 as the state's first public-university CRNA pathway.

Oklahoma City University

Oklahoma City University's Doctor of Nurse Anesthesia Practice, offered through the Kramer School of Nursing, is one of the longest-established CRNA programs in the region. It runs a roughly 36-month curriculum of about 90 to 100 credit hours, combining didactic instruction, simulation, and supervised clinical rotations at major Oklahoma City-area facilities, and it emphasizes early, high-volume clinical immersion. For applicants who value a long track record and a deep clinical pipeline in the OKC metro, it's the anchor program of the state.

University of Tulsa

The University of Tulsa offers a COA-accredited Doctor of Nursing Practice in nurse anesthesia and is the nurse anesthesia program based in the Tulsa metro. Its accreditation is current with a reaccreditation review scheduled by October 2026 — a routine cycle rather than a red flag, but worth confirming directly with COA if your application timeline overlaps that window. Tulsa is the natural in-state choice for applicants rooted in eastern Oklahoma.

University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center

OUHSC's nurse anesthesia track within its Doctor of Nursing Practice is the newest of the three and a milestone for the state: approved in 2025, it is the first CRNA pathway offered by a public university in Oklahoma, created specifically to help close the state's anesthesia-provider gap. Students complete advanced coursework alongside supervised clinical experience at OUHSC's health-system affiliates in the Oklahoma City area. As a newer public option, it may offer a different cost structure than private programs — verify current tuition directly.

From two to three. The arrival of OUHSC's public program meaningfully widens Oklahoma's CRNA pipeline. More accredited seats in-state improves the odds for residents, but every one of these cohorts is still small and competitive — "more programs" doesn't mean "easy admission."

How to Choose

Oklahoma City University is the established private anchor with early clinical immersion; OUHSC is the new public option in the same metro, likely with a different cost profile; the University of Tulsa serves the eastern side of the state. All three are doctorates leading to the same NBCRNA certification exam, so let location, cost, clinical-site geography, and program maturity guide your choice.

Confirm the current numbers. Credit counts, tuition, cohort sizes, and accreditation review dates shift year to year, and OUHSC's program is still young. Use the figures here as a planning baseline and verify each program's current details on its official site and the COA accredited-programs list before applying.

Application Strategy for Oklahoma

Three accredited programs is better odds than most states, but cohorts remain small, so apply broadly: consider all three Oklahoma programs plus neighboring options in Texas and Kansas. Each expects a strong GPA and at least a year of adult ICU experience, and most CRNA programs prefer or require the CCRN — address that early with the CCRN guide.

Bottom Line

Oklahoma went from two CRNA programs to three: Oklahoma City University's established DNAP, the University of Tulsa's DNP, and OUHSC's new public DNP pathway. Apply broadly across all three plus neighboring states, verify each program's current figures, and weigh cost and location over prestige.
