Best Journals for Operating Room (OR) and Perioperative Nurses in 2026

Last updated: June 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes

The operating room is a specialty that rewards precision, documentation, and systematic thinking. OR nurses manage everything from instrument counts to patient safety time-outs — in environments where a missed step can have irreversible consequences. The professionals who excel in perioperative nursing are system-builders. Good journals are part of that system.

Quick picks: Case documentation → OR Circulator Case Log. Certification → CNOR Exam Prep Journal. Career → Perioperative Specialty Skills Tracker.

1. OR Circulator Case Log

Perioperative RNs accumulate diverse case experience across surgical specialties — general, ortho, neuro, cardiac, vascular, plastics, robotics. Without documentation, this experience exists only in your memory. An OR case log captures: case type, surgeon, patient ASA class, anesthesia type, key safety events, instrument count accuracy, and clinical pearls learned. Essential for building a specialty portfolio and CNOR exam preparation. View our OR Circulator RN Case Log.

2. CNOR Exam Preparation Journal

The Certified Perioperative Nurse (CNOR) credential requires 2+ years of perioperative experience and passing a comprehensive exam. A CNOR study journal organizes content domains (patient safety, equipment and supplies, asepsis, communication, emergency situations, nursing practice) with study session logs, high-yield concept summaries, and practice question tracking. Search Amazon for study resources.

3. Surgical Instrument Count and Setup Journal

OR nurses responsible for surgical counts — sponges, sharps, instruments — maintain patient safety through rigorous protocol. A personal reference journal that logs case-specific setup preferences by surgeon (preferences for retractor types, suture choices, positioning equipment, closing materials) dramatically improves case efficiency and surgeon satisfaction. Search Amazon.

4. Perioperative Quality Improvement Journal

OR nurses frequently participate in surgical quality initiatives — wrong-site surgery prevention, fire safety protocols, SSI reduction bundles. Keeping a QI journal tracks: metric baseline, intervention implemented, follow-up measurement, PI meeting notes, and case reports involving safety concerns. This is clinical ladder gold and positions you for charge or coordinator roles. View our Quality Improvement RN Log.

5. Career Advancement Roadmap Journal for OR Nurses

OR nursing offers distinct career paths: scrub specialization, charge RN, OR manager, RNFA (First Assistant), or transition to surgical NP/PA. A career roadmap journal identifies your target role, skills gaps, certification requirements, mentorship relationships, and 3/5/10-year milestones. The OR nurses who advance have a plan; the ones who plateau typically don't. Search Amazon.
