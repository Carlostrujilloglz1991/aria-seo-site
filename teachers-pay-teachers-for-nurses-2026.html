Teachers Pay Teachers for Nurses 2026: How to Sell Nursing Education Resources

Teachers Pay Teachers (TPT) has more than 8 million educators buying and selling resources — and nursing educators are a fast-growing segment. If you teach in a nursing program, precept new grads, or create clinical training materials, TPT is an underutilized income stream with relatively low competition compared to Etsy.

This guide covers how nurses and nursing educators can use TPT in 2026 to sell clinical resources, NCLEX prep materials, simulation scenarios, and continuing education content.

The opportunity: Nursing faculty, clinical preceptors, and nurse educators spend significant time creating materials that don't exist anywhere else. TPT lets you monetize those materials once instead of recreating them for every class.

Many nurse sellers list on both platforms. A simulation scenario sells well on TPT; an ICU hemodynamics pocket card sells better on Etsy. Use both to maximize reach.

Getting Started on TPT

  1. Create your seller account at teacherspayteachers.com. Choose the Premium Seller plan ($59.99/year) over the Basic plan — it gives you 80% royalty vs. 55%, which pays for itself quickly.
  2. Set up your store with a professional name that reflects your niche. Something like "CriticalCareNurseResources" or "NursingClinicalVault" performs better than a personal name.
  3. Create your first resource. Start with something you already have: a clinical checklist, a case study you use for orientation, a drug calculation practice set. Export as PDF.
  4. Write your listing description with specific keywords nursing educators search for: "nursing simulation scenario," "NCLEX practice questions," "clinical rotation evaluation," etc.
  5. Price strategically. Bundle related resources (e.g., 5 NCLEX practice question sets by system) at 20–30% off the individual prices. Bundles drive significantly higher average revenue.

What Makes a TPT Resource Sell

Specificity beats breadth

"Cardiac NCLEX Practice Questions: 50 Question Set with Rationales and SATA" outperforms "Nursing Practice Questions" in search and in conversion. The more specific your product, the clearer the value to the buyer.

NGN format is the current demand driver

The Next Generation NCLEX (NGN) launched in 2023 with new question types that many nursing programs haven't fully integrated yet. Resources that specifically address NGN case study questions, matrix multiple response, and clinical judgment items are in high demand and short supply.

Preview quality drives sales

TPT lets buyers preview the first few pages of your resource. Make your preview look professional — clean formatting, readable fonts, and immediately useful content on page one. Buyers who preview and trust the quality buy more readily.

Income Potential

TPT nursing seller income varies enormously:

  • Casual sellers (5–10 resources): $50–$200/month
  • Active sellers (20–50 resources): $300–$1,000/month
  • Top sellers (100+ resources, bundles): $2,000–$5,000+/month

The key driver is the number of listings and the quality of your bundles. One well-made $35 bundle of 10 NCLEX question sets can outsell 20 individual $4 resources.

Quick win: If you already have Etsy listings or Gumroad PDFs of nursing content, many of those files can be listed on TPT with minimal modification. The same ICU drug reference card that sells on Etsy for $7 can sell on TPT to nurse educators for $10–$15 when positioned as a teaching aid.
