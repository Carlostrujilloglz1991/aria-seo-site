Standard Precautions

Standard precautions apply to ALL patients, regardless of diagnosis or infection status, at ALL times. They are based on the principle that all blood, body fluids, secretions, excretions (except sweat), non-intact skin, and mucous membranes may contain transmissible infectious agents.

  • Hand hygiene (before and after every patient contact; before PPE don; after PPE doff)
  • Gloves for contact with blood, body fluids, secretions, mucous membranes, non-intact skin
  • Gown for splash/spray risk or contact with blood/body fluids
  • Mask and eye protection for splash/spray to face
  • Safe sharps handling (NEVER recap needles two-handed; use safety devices; sharps containers at point of care)
  • Safe injection practices (sterile single-use equipment for injections; no multi-dose vials for multiple patients)
  • Respiratory hygiene (cough etiquette — cover mouth/nose; provide masks to coughing patients)

Airborne precautions require a NEGATIVE PRESSURE room with the door CLOSED. If a negative pressure room is unavailable, patient should be placed in a room with door closed and minimal traffic while transfer is arranged. An N95 respirator (NOT a surgical mask) is required — fit testing is mandatory before use.

PPE: Donning and Doffing Order

Donning Order (GOWN → MASK → GOGGLES → GLOVES)

  1. Perform hand hygiene
  2. Gown: Tie at neck and back; cover arms and torso completely
  3. Mask or respirator: Surgical mask (ties at top/bottom) or N95 (pull-on elastic); mold to nose bridge; perform seal check for N95
  4. Eye protection: Goggles or face shield over top of mask/respirator
  5. Gloves: Don last; pull cuffs over gown sleeves

Doffing Order (GLOVES → GOGGLES → GOWN → MASK)

Doffing is the HIGHER RISK step — self-contamination most likely during removal:

  1. Gloves first: Remove inside-out (grab outside of one glove and pull off; slide finger under inside of remaining glove and pull off); dispose
  2. Perform hand hygiene
  3. Goggles/face shield: Remove by grasping the back strap/earpieces (not the front which is contaminated); dispose or place in designated reprocessing container
  4. Gown: Unfasten ties; pull forward and away, rolling it inside-out; avoid touching front; dispose
  5. Perform hand hygiene
  6. Mask: Remove by grasping ties/elastics at the back (not the front); dispose; do NOT touch mask surface
  7. Perform hand hygiene

Hand Hygiene: The Single Most Effective Infection Control Intervention

Indications (WHO 5 Moments)

  1. BEFORE touching a patient
  2. BEFORE clean/aseptic procedures
  3. AFTER body fluid exposure risk
  4. AFTER touching a patient
  5. AFTER touching patient surroundings

NCLEX High-Yield Points

  • Airborne = TB, Measles, Varicella, disseminated Zoster; requires N95 + NEGATIVE PRESSURE room
  • Droplet = flu, meningococcal, pertussis, mumps; surgical mask + private room + door closed
  • Contact = MRSA, VRE, C. diff, RSV, scabies, norovirus; gown + gloves upon room entry
  • C. diff: SOAP AND WATER only — ABHR does NOT kill spores; bleach for environmental cleaning
  • PPE donning order: Gown → Mask → Goggles → Gloves. Doffing: Gloves → Goggles → Gown → Mask
  • WHO 5 Moments: before touching patient, before clean procedure, after body fluid exposure, after touching patient, after touching patient surroundings
  • MRSA treatment: vancomycin IV (systemic); mupirocin intranasal for decolonization
  • Negative pressure room: air flows IN from hallway; exhaust goes OUT or is HEPA filtered; for airborne precautions
  • Highest risk moment for self-contamination: DOFFING — most infections from improper removal of contaminated PPE
