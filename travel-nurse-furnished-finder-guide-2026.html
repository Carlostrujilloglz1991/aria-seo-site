Furnished Finder for Travel Nurses 2026: The Complete Guide

Part of the Travel Nursing Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Furnished Finder is the #1 platform specifically for travel nurse housing. Here's how to use it effectively — and what to do when it doesn't have options in your assignment city.

What Is Furnished Finder?

Furnished Finder is a housing marketplace specifically designed for travel healthcare workers. Unlike Airbnb, it focuses on monthly rentals (most travel contracts are 13 weeks), landlords understand travel nursing income patterns, and listings are typically unfurnished-to-fully-furnished apartments and rooms near hospital campuses.

How to Search Effectively

Search 4–6 weeks before your contract start date. Filter by: your assignment city + radius, move-in date that matches your start, and "travel nurse friendly" or "healthcare worker" tags. Set up email alerts for new listings — the best units go within 24–48 hours of posting.

Negotiating Monthly Rates

Furnished Finder landlords post prices, but most are open to negotiation, especially mid-week postings that haven't received interest. Common negotiation levers: offering to pay 2–3 months upfront in exchange for a 10% discount, agreeing to a longer stay (extend option) in exchange for reduced rate, or waiving demands for weekly cleaning in exchange for lower rent.

Negotiation script: "I'm a travel nurse starting at [Hospital] on [date]. I have excellent references from my last 3 assignments. I'm interested in your listing at $X/month — would you consider $Y if I paid the first two months upfront?"

Red Flags and Scams

Watch for: listings with no verified landlord badge, requests to move communication off-platform to email/text before any agreement, prices that are 40%+ below market rate for the area, landlords who "require" payment via wire transfer or Zelle before you've seen the unit, and listings with stock photos (reverse image search them).

When Furnished Finder Has Nothing

For rural assignments or low-inventory markets: Airbnb monthly discount (book 28+ days for 20–40% off nightly rate), Vrbo monthly, Furnished Finder's "Request Board" (post your needs, landlords reach out to you), local Facebook groups for travel nurses in that city, and your agency's housing department (they often have a Rolodex of local options even if you take the stipend).
