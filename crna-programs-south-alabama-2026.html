University of South Alabama CRNA Program: 2026 Overview

The University of South Alabama (USA), based in Mobile, runs one of the larger and better-known nurse anesthesia programs in the Southeast through its College of Nursing. It's a public university, which makes it a frequent point of comparison for cost-conscious applicants weighing private programs. For ICU nurses in the Gulf South and beyond who are mapping a path to CRNA, South Alabama is a name worth understanding. Here's a practical, applicant-focused overview for 2026.

Verify the details before you apply. Program length, tuition, prerequisites, and admissions criteria change year to year and by cohort, and some formats may be delivered in a distance-accessible or hybrid structure. Treat everything below as an orientation, not a source of truth — confirm current specifics directly with the University of South Alabama's nurse anesthesia program office before making decisions.

Program format and degree

Like every US nurse anesthesia program today, South Alabama's culminates in a doctoral credential (a DNP-level nurse anesthesia degree), in line with the national requirement that all new CRNAs graduate at the doctoral level. Programs of this type generally run about 36 months of full-time study — front-loaded didactic coursework followed by an intensive clinical residency across affiliated hospital and surgical sites. South Alabama has historically been known for a model that reaches students across a wide geographic area, with clinical placements distributed among partner facilities; confirm the current delivery format and residency-site expectations directly, since these details shape where you'll actually live during training.

Typical prerequisites

Nurse anesthesia admissions requirements are fairly standardized across programs. For South Alabama, expect the core elements common to competitive CRNA schools:

  • An unencumbered RN license and a BSN (or equivalent) from an accredited program.
  • A minimum of one year — realistically more — of recent, full-time critical care experience in an adult ICU managing vasoactive drips, ventilators, and invasive lines.
  • A competitive GPA, with science and graduate coursework weighted heavily; many admitted applicants exceed a 3.5.
  • GRE requirements that vary by cycle — confirm whether the current year requires, recommends, or waives it.
  • CCRN certification, strong references, a personal statement, and a formal interview.
  • CRNA shadowing hours, and sometimes prerequisite graduate-level science depending on the year.

Admissions competitiveness

CRNA admission is competitive everywhere. As a well-established public program, South Alabama draws a large, national applicant pool, and the differentiators are the standard ones: the acuity of your ICU experience (a high-acuity unit — CVICU, medical/surgical ICU, or trauma — reads stronger than a step-down), CCRN certification, an upward GPA trend, genuine understanding of the role through shadowing, and a strong interview. Because public-program tuition is more accessible, these seats can attract heavy interest, so treat every part of the application as a differentiator.

Cost and financing

As a public university, South Alabama's tuition is generally lower than comparable private CRNA programs — a meaningful advantage given that nurse anesthesia is a multi-year, largely income-free commitment. That said, total cost still runs well into the five or six figures once fees, living costs during the clinical phase, travel to distributed clinical sites, and lost wages are all counted. In-state versus out-of-state status can change the math significantly, so confirm your residency category. Most students combine federal graduate loans with savings.
