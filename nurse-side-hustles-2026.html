Best Side Hustles for Nurses in 2026 (Beyond Per Diem)

Beyond the obvious: Per diem shifts are the default nurse side hustle and they work. But per diem is still trading hours for dollars. The side hustles here either leverage your nursing license for premium rates, create scalable income, or build toward financial independence in ways that per diem alone cannot.

Nurses are one of the most entrepreneurially underserved professional groups. You have high-demand clinical skills, a professional license that opens doors most people cannot enter, and direct experience with healthcare delivery from bedside to systems. Yet nurse culture has historically underemphasized income diversification and side business development. That is changing — partly because nurse wages have not kept pace with cost of living in many markets, and partly because the burnout epidemic has created economic pressure to generate more income with fewer clinical hours. These are the side hustles worth knowing about in 2026.

1. Legal Nurse Consulting ($75-150/hour)

Legal nurse consulting is one of the highest-hourly-rate side hustles available to experienced nurses. Law firms handling medical malpractice, personal injury, and workers' compensation cases hire legal nurse consultants to review medical records, interpret clinical documentation, identify standard-of-care issues, and educate attorneys about healthcare processes. The work is done independently on your own schedule — reviewing records and writing reports between shifts or on days off. Rates range from $75-$150/hour, with expert witness work paying significantly more.

The American Association of Legal Nurse Consultants (AALNC) certification (LNCC) is available for nurses with five or more years of clinical experience and strengthens credibility with law firms. To get started, contact plaintiff and defense medical malpractice firms in your area and offer record review services at an introductory rate while building a portfolio of cases.

2. Telehealth Nursing ($35-65/hour)

Telehealth nursing has sustained significantly higher volumes than pre-pandemic levels and continues to grow. Platforms including Teladoc, MDLive, Wheel, and Amazon Clinic hire RNs and NPs for patient triage, chronic disease management support, and post-discharge follow-up on flexible 4-hour block schedules that are fully remote. For nurses who want to generate supplemental income on off days or between travel contracts without the physical demands of bedside work, telehealth is the most accessible option. Rates are $35-45/hour for bedside RNs, $55-90/hour for NPs with advanced practice licensure.

3. CE Course Development (Royalties + Hourly)

Nursing continuing education is a multi-billion-dollar annual industry. Platforms including Nurse.com, Relias, and Healthstream pay content developers to create accredited CE courses on clinical topics. If you have deep specialty expertise — ECMO management, CRNA pharmacology, sepsis protocols, trauma nursing — you can develop content that generates ongoing royalty income from nurses completing your course. The upfront investment is 40-80 hours of development work; the income continues passively for years afterward. A popular CE course on a high-demand topic can generate $5,000-$20,000 per year in royalties with no ongoing effort after initial approval.

4. Nurse Content Creation (Variable, Scalable)

Nurse-to-nurse content — clinical education, career guidance, financial advice, travel nursing reviews — reaches a large and engaged audience that healthcare brands and CE companies pay to access. Nurses who build YouTube channels, Instagram followings, or blogs on nursing topics can monetize through brand partnerships, affiliate marketing, and sponsored content. The realistic timeline: 12-18 months of consistent content production before meaningful income begins, with 2-3 years before the business becomes self-sustaining. AI writing tools have reduced the per-piece content creation time substantially, making this more realistic for working nurses than it was three years ago.

5. NCLEX Tutoring ($60-120/hour)

Since the Next Generation NCLEX (NGN) rollout, nursing students have struggled with the new case study-based format and pay premium rates for one-on-one guidance from clinical nurses who understand how to think through NGN-style questions. NCLEX tutoring is flexible — fully online, on your schedule — and the $60-120/hour rate makes it one of the most efficient uses of nursing knowledge outside a clinical setting. Start by posting in nursing school Facebook groups and listing on tutoring platforms like Wyzant. Word of mouth from successful students drives new clients quickly.

6. Case Management and Utilization Review Consulting

Health insurance companies and hospital systems regularly hire contract nurses for utilization review, appeals processing, and complex case management on a per-case or per-hour basis. Rates are $45-85/hour with fully remote work schedules. Experienced case management nurses or nurses with UR backgrounds can build a contract client base across multiple insurance companies, generating supplemental income on evenings and weekends that scales with available time.

7. Nursing School Clinical Adjunct Faculty

Clinical adjunct nursing faculty positions pay $25-60/hour and leverage the clinical work you are already doing — patient care — with the addition of student supervision. For nurses who enjoy teaching and mentoring, this is among the most natural side income options because the marginal effort of supervising students while delivering care you would deliver anyway is much lower than starting a business from scratch. Contact nursing programs in your area about adjunct clinical faculty openings, particularly if you work at a hospital with an affiliated nursing program.

Best Side Hustles for Nurses in 2026 (That Actually Pay)

Most "nurse side hustle" lists are full of survey sites and resume-writing gigs that pay $15 an hour. This isn't that. If you're a nurse with a license, clinical experience, and the ability to communicate clearly under pressure, you're sitting on skills the market will pay a premium for -- you just need to know where to look.

Here are the side hustles nurses are using in 2026 to generate real supplemental income, ranked roughly by earning potential and startup ease.

1. Telehealth and Virtual Triage -- $35-$85/hr

The telehealth industry hasn't stopped growing since the pandemic normalized virtual care. Platforms like Nurse Wise, Fonemed, and TriageLogic hire RNs on a per-call or hourly contract basis. You work from home, set your availability, and handle inbound patient triage calls.

What it pays

Most telehealth nurse contracts start at $35-$45/hr for inbound triage. With experience and specialization (pediatrics, oncology follow-up), rates can reach $75-$85/hr. A 15-hour week adds $500-$1,200 to your take-home.

How to start

  • Search "telehealth triage RN" on Indeed or LinkedIn -- platforms post openings constantly
  • Prepare a home workspace with HIPAA-compliant setup (private room, secure Wi-Fi, headset)
  • You'll need an active multi-state license or compact RN license for most platforms

2. Legal Nurse Consulting -- $100-$200/hr

Attorneys handling medical malpractice, personal injury, and workers' compensation cases need nurses to translate medical records into plain language. This is one of the highest-paid uses of clinical knowledge outside of direct patient care.

What it pays

Entry-level legal nurse consultants bill $50-$75/hr. Established LNCs with a track record charge $100-$200/hr for record review, report writing, and deposition prep. A single complex case can generate $2,000-$8,000 in fees.

How to start

The American Association of Legal Nurse Consultants (AALNC) offers training and certification. The CLNC certification from Vickie Milazzo Institute is widely recognized. Budget $2,000-$5,000 for training -- most nurses recoup this in their first case.

3. Nurse Educator / Clinical Faculty (Part-Time) -- $45-$95/hr

Community colleges and nursing programs regularly hire part-time clinical instructors and simulation lab faculty. If you have a BSN (and preferably an MSN), this is a natural extension of what you already know.

What it pays

Part-time clinical instructor pay varies by institution: $45-$65/hr at community colleges, up to $85-$95/hr at private nursing schools. Teaching one clinical section per semester (8-12 hours/week) adds $12,000-$25,000 annually.

Compact license advantage: Nurses with NLC compact licenses can take on telehealth and education contracts across state lines without additional licensure fees -- a major cost and paperwork advantage.

4. Healthcare Writing and Content Creation -- $50-$150/hr

Hospitals, health insurers, pharmaceutical companies, and digital health startups need nurses who can write. Medical content that's accurate AND readable is rare. If you can do it, you can charge for it.

What it pays

Freelance health writers with clinical credentials charge $0.25-$1.00 per word for articles, or $75-$150/hr for project-rate work. Patient education materials, blog content, and insurance documentation all pay well.

Where to find clients

  • Contena and Mediabistro for health writing jobs
  • Direct outreach to hospital marketing departments
  • LinkedIn -- "medical writer RN" is a searchable credential that attracts inbound interest

5. Wellness and Corporate Health Coaching -- $75-$200/hr

Companies are paying for on-site and virtual wellness programs. Nurses with additional coaching certifications (ICF-accredited or ACE Health Coach) can sell packages to HR departments, small businesses, and individuals managing chronic conditions.

What it pays

Individual coaching packages run $500-$2,000 per client. Corporate wellness consulting can generate $3,000-$10,000 per contract. This income is location-independent and schedule-flexible.

6. Selling Digital Products -- Passive Income After Setup

Nurses are uniquely positioned to create and sell educational resources: study guides, clinical checklists, shift organization templates, NCLEX prep materials, nurse planner PDFs. Once created, these products earn while you sleep.

What it pays

A well-positioned digital product on a platform like Gumroad or Etsy can earn $500-$5,000/month with no ongoing labor. The key is solving a specific problem nurses actually face -- not another generic template.

How to Choose the Right Side Hustle

The right side hustle depends on your specialty, schedule, and income goal. If you need income fast, telehealth and per-diem shifts are the quickest path. If you want to build something scalable that doesn't require trading hours for dollars, digital products and coaching are where to focus.

The nurses who build real financial independence don't pick one thing and grind it forever. They start with one income stream, systematize it, then layer in a second. Within 12-18 months, many are earning an extra $2,000-$5,000/month outside their hospital job -- enough to cut hours, eliminate debt faster, or build a real emergency fund.
