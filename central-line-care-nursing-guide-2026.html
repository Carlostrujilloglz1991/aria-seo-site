Central line-associated bloodstream infections (CLABSI) kill approximately 28,000 ICU patients annually and cost over $46,000 per episode. Most CLABSIs are preventable with consistent adherence to the central line bundle.

CLABSI Prevention Bundle (CDC/IHI)

The central line bundle has been shown to reduce CLABSI rates by up to 66% when all elements are followed consistently.

Central Line Dressing Change Protocol

Supplies

  • Sterile gloves (two pairs)
  • Mask (and one for patient/visitors)
  • CHG-impregnated sponge disk (Biopatch) — placed around insertion site
  • CHG swabsticks or applicator (2% CHG / 70% IPA)
  • Transparent semipermeable membrane (TSM) dressing
  • Sterile drape
  • New catheter securement device (StatLock or sutureless device)

Procedure

  1. Perform hand hygiene; don mask; have patient put on mask
  2. Don non-sterile gloves; remove old dressing by lifting edges and peeling toward the insertion site (avoids catheter displacement)
  3. Inspect site: redness, swelling, tenderness, exudate, skin breakdown; measure/document catheter external length if applicable
  4. Remove non-sterile gloves; hand hygiene; don sterile gloves
  5. Clean insertion site with CHG applicator using back-and-forth friction for 30 seconds; allow to dry 30 seconds (critical — CHG kills bacteria on drying)
  6. Apply CHG disk (Biopatch) foam side down directly over the insertion site
  7. Apply new securement device if needed
  8. Apply TSM dressing; ensure all edges are sealed; no wrinkles (wrinkles harbor bacteria)
  9. Label dressing with date, time, and initials
  10. Document dressing change, site assessment, and patient tolerance
Never use scissors near central lines — accidental catheter transection can cause air embolism or loss of the line. Use blunt-ended scissors only if required, with extreme caution.

Push-Pause Flushing Technique

  • Flush with 1–2 mL bursts, pausing briefly between each push
  • Creates turbulent flow inside the catheter lumen, removing debris and preventing biofilm buildup
  • NEVER force a flush against resistance — fibrin sheath or clot; forcing can dislodge a clot or rupture the catheter
  • End with positive pressure: maintain thumb pressure on plunger while withdrawing the syringe OR use positive-pressure cap

Daily Line Assessment

  • Necessity review: Does this patient still need this line? Document yes/no every shift
  • Site inspection: Erythema, warmth, swelling, tenderness, purulent drainage = signs of infection; notify provider
  • Dressing integrity: Adherent on all edges, dry, not soiled, label visible; change if compromised
  • Securement device: Not pinching skin, not displaced; ensure catheter is not kinked or pulled
  • External catheter length: Compare to documented insertion length; migration in or out indicates catheter displacement
  • All lumens patent: Flush each lumen; document blood return if required per policy
  • Infusion compatibility: Y-site compatibility checked for all simultaneous infusions

NCLEX High-Yield Points

  • CLABSI bundle: hand hygiene + maximal sterile barrier + CHG antisepsis + optimal site + daily necessity review + scrub the hub + dressing maintenance
  • Subclavian preferred over femoral (lowest infection + DVT risk)
  • CHG must DRY before applying dressing — drying is the bactericidal mechanism
  • Scrub the hub: ≥15 seconds before every access, every time — this is the #1 CLABSI prevention intervention nurses control
  • TSM dressing: change every 7 days; gauze dressing: every 48 hours; change immediately if wet, soiled, or lifted
  • Air embolism: left lateral decubitus + Trendelenburg; 100% O2; notify provider STAT
  • NEVER force a flush against resistance — risk of catheter rupture or clot dislodgement
  • Hemodialysis catheter: do NOT use for other medications/fluids without a specific order
