CRNA Salary by State 2026

Where you practice as a CRNA matters almost as much as the credential itself. State practice laws, cost of living, and market demand create salary ranges that vary by $50,000–$100,000 for the same job title.

National median (2026): $214,000. Top 10% earns $290,000+. Independent contractors and locums CRNAs routinely clear $300,000–$450,000 annually. The floor is around $160,000 in low-demand supervision-required states. Geography and practice setting determine where in that range you land.

Independent Practice vs. Supervision — Why It Matters Financially

In the 22+ states that have opted out of Medicare's physician supervision requirement for CRNAs, nurse anesthetists can open independent practices, bill directly under their own NPI, and take on locums contracts without a supervising physician. This is the income ceiling difference. A CRNA in Wyoming can open a solo anesthesia practice and bill $350,000–$500,000/year in collections, keeping the business margin after overhead. In a mandatory supervision state, that model doesn't exist — you must work within a physician-supervised environment.

For hospital employment, the supervision vs. independent distinction matters less — hospital-employed CRNAs in supervision states still earn $180,000–$230,000 on salary. The income difference becomes significant when you consider independent contracting, locums, and eventually owning your own practice.

The Locums Premium

CRNA locums rates in 2026 range from $175–$250/hour for short-term contracts. At 40 hours/week, 48 weeks/year, that's $336,000–$480,000 gross. Locums CRNAs are self-employed and pay both sides of FICA (15.3% on the first $168,600), but they also qualify for the 20% qualified business income deduction and can contribute to a solo 401(k) at up to $70,000/year. Net-of-tax locums income at $400,000 gross can exceed the net from $250,000 hospital salary after accounting for retirement contribution differences.

Arizona Specifically

Arizona is an independent practice state — CRNAs practice without mandatory physician supervision. The Phoenix and Tucson markets are growing, driven by population growth and healthcare system expansion. Starting hospital salaries for new CRNA graduates in Arizona run $195,000–$215,000. Experienced CRNAs at 3–5 years earn $215,000–$240,000. The locums market in Arizona runs $185–$225/hour for short-term contracts. Arizona's 4.5% flat state income tax (one of the lowest among major-market states) improves the net income picture versus California at 9.3–13.3%.

The state selection strategy for new CRNA graduates: Year 1–3 in a high-salary state to pay down school debt aggressively, then reassess location based on lifestyle, family, and independent practice goals. A CRNA earning $228,000 in Arizona with no state income tax on most retirement contributions and a 3-year aggressive debt paydown plan is in a financially stronger position than one earning $248,000 in California paying 9.3% state income tax on the same income.
