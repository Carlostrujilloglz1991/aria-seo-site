The 2026 NCLEX-RN: Why ATI's Clinical Judgment Focus Matters

The 2023 NGN transition fundamentally changed what the NCLEX tests. The old NCLEX rewarded memorization of isolated facts ("what is the antidote for heparin?"). The new NCLEX tests clinical judgment — the nurse's ability to recognize cues, analyze information, prioritize hypotheses, generate solutions, and evaluate outcomes across multi-patient case studies.

This is exactly where ATI's curriculum was built. ATI partnered with the NCSBN (the organization that writes the NCLEX) to align their content to the Clinical Judgment Measurement Model decades before NGN launched. That alignment shows:

  • ATI's case studies mirror the 6-item NGN format exactly
  • ATI's rationales explicitly name the clinical judgment cognitive skill being tested
  • ATI's content modules are organized around recognition, analysis, and action — not just "know this fact"

ATI's Content Mastery Series: What Nursing Schools Use
