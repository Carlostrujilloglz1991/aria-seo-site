CRNA Programs in North Dakota

Part of the CRNA Career Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

North Dakota has exactly one accredited nurse anesthesia program — the University of North Dakota's DNP in Nurse Anesthesia in Grand Forks. It's an established, fully accredited option, but with a single program in the state, in-state applicants should plan to apply across the region as well.

The landscape: North Dakota has a single COA-accredited nurse anesthesia program — the University of North Dakota College of Nursing & Professional Disciplines in Grand Forks. With one program serving the state, applicants should build a regional list alongside their UND application.

The University of North Dakota runs the only nurse anesthesia program in the state, offered through its College of Nursing as a full-time, 36-month Doctor of Nursing Practice track. It is accredited by the Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Educational Programs (COA) through 2031 and trains students to the national clinical standard — a minimum of 2,000 clinical hours and 600 cases — required to sit for the certification exam.

Admission Requirements

UND follows the national CRNA template. You will need a baccalaureate degree or higher from an accredited nursing program, a current unencumbered U.S. RN license in good standing, a minimum of one year of full-time critical-care experience, and a minimum 3.0 GPA. As everywhere, the ICU requirement is non-negotiable, and competitive applicants typically bring more than the minimum year, high-acuity experience, and the CCRN credential — see the CCRN guide. For the full checklist, review the general CRNA school requirements.

One program in the state means you should apply widely. With a single cohort in North Dakota, treating UND as your only target is risky. Build a regional list that includes nearby states with established programs — Minnesota, South Dakota, and Iowa are all within reasonable reach and expand your odds considerably.
Confirm the current numbers. Cohort size, tuition, total credit count, and admission rules can change year to year. Treat the figures here as a planning baseline and verify UND's current details on its official program site and the COA accredited-programs list before applying.

Application Strategy for North Dakota

Because North Dakota has a single program, your plan should be regional from day one. Apply to UND, but build a list that also includes Minnesota, South Dakota, and Wisconsin to give yourself realistic odds across a competitive applicant pool. Strengthen your file with the required ICU year (ideally more), the CCRN, and a sharp personal statement. For realistic expectations, see CRNA acceptance rates.

Bottom Line

The University of North Dakota in Grand Forks is the state's only accredited CRNA program: a three-year, COA-accredited DNP in Nurse Anesthesia with accreditation secured through 2031. It's a solid, established option, but with one cohort in the state, North Dakota applicants should apply regionally to give themselves a fair shot.
