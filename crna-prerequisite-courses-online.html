CRNA Prerequisite Courses Online 2026 — Finish Your Prereqs Without Overpaying

Part of the CRNA Career Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

Prerequisite gaps stall a lot of CRNA applications — a missing or outdated chemistry, biochemistry, or statistics course. The good news: you can often complete these affordably online without a full degree program. Here's how to do it right and avoid wasting money on credits that don't transfer.

The short version: Confirm each target program's exact prerequisite list and recency rules first, then complete the gaps through accredited community colleges or reputable online course providers — often at a fraction of university tuition. The one rule that saves money: verify a course will be accepted before you pay and enroll.

Which prerequisites programs commonly want

Requirements vary, but CRNA programs frequently look for coursework such as chemistry (sometimes organic and/or biochemistry), statistics, and health-assessment or physiology-related courses. Some programs also care about recency — a chemistry course from many years ago may need to be retaken. Because there's no universal list, your first step is to open each target program's admissions page and write down exactly what they require, at what level, and how recent it must be.

Where to take them affordably

Community colleges are often the cheapest route, and many now offer science and statistics courses online. Accredited online course providers that specialize in prerequisite coursework for health-professions applicants are another common option — convenient and self-paced, though prices vary. University extension or continuing-education programs can work too. The cost difference between these and full university tuition can be substantial, which is why so many applicants complete prereqs this way.

The transferability rule that saves money

Verify acceptance before you pay. The most expensive mistake is completing a course that a program won't accept — wrong level, non-accredited provider, missing a required lab, or not counting because it was pass/fail. Email the program's admissions office with the specific course and provider and ask plainly whether it satisfies the requirement. Get the answer before enrolling. A five-minute email can save you hundreds of dollars and a wasted term.

Labs, level, and grades

Watch three details. Labs: some science prerequisites must include a lab component — a lecture-only course may not count. Level: confirm whether a program wants an introductory or an upper-level version (for example, a rigorous biochemistry rather than a survey course). Grades: prerequisites usually need a solid grade, and CRNA admissions are competitive on GPA, so aim to actually do well — a barely-passed prereq helps less than a strong one.

Sequencing and timing

Fit prereqs into your application timeline so they're finished (with grades posted and transcripts sendable) before deadlines. Online and self-paced formats help you do this alongside full-time ICU work, but don't underestimate a demanding science course while working nights — give yourself realistic runway, and don't stack a heavy prereq load into the same weeks you're writing essays and chasing references.

Bottom line: You can complete CRNA prerequisites affordably online — but only if you confirm each program's exact requirements and verify transferability before paying. Favor accredited community colleges and reputable providers, mind labs and course level, earn strong grades, and sequence the work so it finishes before your deadlines. The cheap path only stays cheap if the credits actually count.

Frequently asked questions

Can I take CRNA prerequisites online?
Often yes — through accredited community colleges or reputable online providers, frequently at far lower cost than university tuition. Verify acceptance first.

What's the biggest money-wasting mistake?
Completing a course a program won't accept — wrong level, no lab, or a non-accredited provider. Email admissions to confirm before you enroll and pay.

Do prerequisite grades matter?
Yes. CRNA admissions are competitive on GPA, so aim to earn strong grades, not just pass.
