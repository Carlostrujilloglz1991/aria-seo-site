Nurse Gear Essentials 2026

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

The items that actually matter for a 12-hour ICU or travel nursing shift. Written by someone who has worked nights and learned which gear earns its bag space and which doesn't.

Travel nurse specific note: You are packing this gear into a car or checked luggage every 13 weeks. Weight, durability, and replaceability all matter more than they do for staff nurses. Buy things that survive a move and aren't irreplaceable if lost.

The Non-Negotiables

Stethoscope — Littmann Classic III (~$120) or Cardiology IV (~$200)

The Classic III handles everything most nurses need. The Cardiology IV makes a real difference in noisy ICU environments where subtle cardiac sounds matter. See the full stethoscope comparison for specialty-specific picks. Travel nurses: bring the Classic III, not your best scope.

Nursing Shoes — Clogs or Athletic, Non-Negotiable Support

Dansko Professional clogs (~$130-160) are the gold standard for nurses who stand 12 hours. The wooden clog base distributes weight in a way foam soles do not. Alternatives: Brooks Ghost or HOKA Bondi for nurses who prefer athletic shoes. The wrong shoes cost you $200-600 in orthopedic appointments within 2 years. Get the right shoes first.

Travel nurses: Danskos are worth the checked luggage space. Foot pain on a travel assignment with no familiar PT and no sick time is a serious problem.

Trauma Shears / Bandage Scissors — Prestige Medical (~$6)

Buy 3. Put one in your work bag, one in your scrub pocket, one in your locker. They walk away constantly. Prestige Medical 7.25" are the standard. Not worth spending more than $8/pair because they will disappear.

Penlight with Pupil Gauge — Prestige Medical or ADC (~$8-15)

Hospital-issued penlights run out of battery at the worst moment. Own one. The pupil gauge on the clip is not a convenience feature in neuro ICU -- it is part of your neurological assessment tool. Replace the battery at the start of every 13-week contract.

Nursing Bag — Tumi Alpha or Nurse Mates Clinical (~$60-350)

Two philosophies: buy a cheap nursing bag you don't mind replacing, or buy one quality bag that lasts 5+ years. The Nurse Mates Napa is a durable mid-tier option (~$80). The Tumi Alpha Bravo is what nurses who are tired of replacing bags eventually buy. Travel nurses benefit from the durable bag because the bag goes through more physical stress than a staff nurse's bag.

ICU-Specific Essentials

Hemostats / Kelly Clamps — 2 pair

For clamping lines, securing Foley bags, a hundred other things nobody teaches you but every ICU nurse develops a use for. Cheap stainless steel, $5-8/pair. Buy from Amazon, not the hospital gift shop.

Black Sharpie (Permanent) + Regular Pen

You will use these 30+ times per shift. Keep one in each scrub pocket. The nurses who are always borrowing pens are the ones who haven't solved this problem yet.

Badge Reel — Retractable, Breakaway

Breakaway badge reels are a safety requirement in most hospital policies (patient safety -- a patient grabs your badge, the reel releases rather than choking you). The $3 badge reels from Amazon break within 2 months. The $12-18 ones from badge reel specialty brands last a year+. Travel nurses: keep a spare reel in your bag because hospital security will not let you past the door without your badge.

Travel Nurse-Specific Gear

Compression Socks — 2-3 pairs per rotation

Figs, Therafirm, or Sockwell -- all work well. 15-20 mmHg compression is the standard nursing level. Travel nurses who are on their feet in an unfamiliar unit with a longer commute and no ergonomic adjustments yet benefit from compression socks more than staff nurses in familiar environments. Start the habit early.

Mini First Aid Kit for the Car/Apartment

You are 13 weeks in a new city without a primary care provider you know. Keep a basic first aid kit, ibuprofen, Benadryl, and your chronic medication supply for at least 30 days past your contract end date in case there is a gap before your next contract starts.

Small Combination Lock

Travel nurses cycle through multiple hospital locker rooms. Not every hospital provides locking lockers. A $12 combination lock (Master Lock 1500iD or similar) means your bag stays secure across 4-6 different facilities over your travel career.
