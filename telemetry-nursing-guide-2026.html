Telemetry Lead Placement

Most telemetry units use a 5-lead system. Standard placement:

ElectrodePlacementColor (US)
RA (Right Arm)Right infraclavicular areaWhite
LA (Left Arm)Left infraclavicular areaBlack
RL (Right Leg)Right lower abdomen/hipGreen
LL (Left Leg)Left lower abdomen/hipRed
V (Chest)V1: 4th ICS right sternal borderBrown/White
Memory aid: "White is right, smoke over fire" — white on right, black (smoke) on left, red (fire) below left.

Normal Sinus Rhythm Criteria

  • Rate: 60–100 bpm
  • Rhythm: Regular (P-P and R-R intervals consistent)
  • P wave: upright in Lead II, one before every QRS
  • PR interval: 0.12–0.20 seconds (3–5 small boxes)
  • QRS: <0.12 seconds (3 small boxes)
  • QT interval: <0.44 sec (men), <0.46 sec (women); varies with rate

Critical Alarm Response

Life-Threatening Alarms — Respond within 30 seconds:
  • Ventricular fibrillation / pulseless VT
  • Asystole
  • HR <30 or >180 bpm sustained
  • Complete heart block (3rd degree)

Alarm Response Steps

  1. Look at monitor — confirm the rhythm (artifact vs true arrhythmia)
  2. Go to bedside immediately — assess patient: responsive? Breathing? Pulse?
  3. If unresponsive/pulseless: call code blue, begin CPR
  4. If responsive: vital signs, O2, 12-lead EKG, notify MD
  5. Document: rhythm, patient response, interventions, time notified
Artifact tip: Patient movement, loose leads, or 60-cycle interference can mimic VF. Always CHECK THE PATIENT before calling a code.

Nursing Interventions by Rhythm

Atrial Fibrillation Nursing Care

  • Assess rate: goal usually 60–110 bpm at rest (rate control)
  • Monitor for thromboembolic events — new neuro deficits, stroke signs
  • Verify anticoagulation order (warfarin INR 2–3 or DOAC)
  • Daily weights and I&O — HF often coexists
  • Hold rate-controlling agents if HR <50 or per parameters
  • Pre-cardioversion: confirm anticoagulation history; NPO if ordered for cardioversion

Pacemaker Patients on Telemetry

  • Identify pacemaker type (pacing leads on rhythm strip — pacing spike before P and/or QRS)
  • Assess for failure to capture (spike without response) or failure to sense (pacer fires when it shouldn't)
  • Set low HR alarm 5–10 bpm below programmed pacemaker rate
  • Avoid MRI unless device is MRI-conditional
  • Document paced vs intrinsic beats

NCLEX High-Yield Points

  • Atropine is FIRST-LINE for symptomatic sinus bradycardia
  • Torsades = long QT + polymorphic VT; treat with Mag Sulfate 2g IV
  • Check PULSE before treating any rhythm — artifact can mimic VF
  • 3rd degree AV block: P waves and QRS are completely independent
  • Digoxin toxicity: bradycardia + N/V + visual changes; hold and notify MD
  • Adenosine must be given as a rapid push closest to IV site followed immediately by NS flush
  • Cardioversion = synchronized (for rhythms with QRS); defibrillation = unsynchronized (for pulseless VT/VF)
