Last updated: June 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes

CRNA school is 24–36 months of the most demanding academic and clinical training in healthcare. Students juggling pharmacokinetics, anesthesia machine check-off, and 60+ clinical hours per week don't have time to browse Amazon. If you know a student in a nurse anesthesia program, here are the gifts that actually help.

Quick picks: Case log → SRNA Anesthesia Case Log. Study tool → Anesthesia Secrets (book). Clinical gear → pocket drug reference app subscription.

1. SRNA Anesthesia Case Log Book ($14–$18)

CRNA programs require students to document hundreds of anesthesia cases for accreditation. The SRNA case log includes: patient ASA classification, anesthesia type (GA, MAC, neuraxial, regional), procedures performed, attending CRNA/physician, case duration, and clinical pearls. This is a professional requirement, not optional.

Digital case logging is increasingly common, but many SRNAs also keep a written backup. Look for a log that includes: case number sequencing, space for GRE drug dosing, and a clinical competency checklist per case. View our SRNA Case Log.

2. Anesthesia Pharmacology Reference Cards

First-year SRNA pharmacology is overwhelming. Students are expected to know the onset, peak, duration, mechanism, and clinical pearls for hundreds of drugs within weeks. Laminated quick-reference cards that organize drugs by class (opioids, benzodiazepines, reversal agents, neuromuscular blocking agents, vasopressors) are invaluable during clinical rotations when you can't pull out a textbook. Search Amazon.

3. Morgan and Mikhail's Clinical Anesthesiology ($60–$70)

Every CRNA student needs this textbook. It's the definitive clinical anesthesiology reference used in most CRNA programs — comprehensive, well-organized, and actually readable. The updated edition covers regional anesthesia, pediatric considerations, and special populations in depth. Search Amazon.

4. CRNA School Application Journal (for prospective SRNAs)

If someone is still in the application phase, a structured CRNA school application journal tracks: ICU hours accumulated, GRE study progress, school application deadlines, LOR request timeline, personal statement drafts, and interview preparation. Most applicants manage this loosely in notes apps and spreadsheets — a dedicated journal keeps the multi-year process coherent. View our CRNA School Application Journal.

5. Quality Noise-Canceling Earbuds for Study ($150–$250)

SRNA students study 4–6 hours per day on top of clinical hours. Quality noise-canceling earbuds (Sony WF-1000XM5, Apple AirPods Pro, Bose QuietComfort) dramatically improve study focus in libraries, coffee shops, and hospital break rooms. This is the one gift where spending more matters. Search Amazon.
