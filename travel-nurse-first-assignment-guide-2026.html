Travel Nurse First Assignment Guide 2026

Part of the Travel Nursing Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Your first travel assignment will feel overwhelming. That's normal. Every experienced travel nurse remembers their first 2 weeks as the hardest — and almost all of them extended their first contract.

Before You Arrive: 2-Week Pre-Assignment Checklist

  • Confirm your start date, unit, and reporting location in writing from your recruiter
  • Submit all compliance paperwork (drug screen, background check, health records, skills checklist) — missing one document can delay your start
  • Confirm housing and get the address; set up utilities if needed
  • Notify your bank you'll be in a new state — prevent card blocks
  • Set up direct deposit with your new agency payroll
  • Get your state licensure confirmed (compact or single-state endorsed)
  • Pack for 13 weeks + professional clothes for orientation week

Orientation: What to Expect

Most hospitals provide 2–4 days of general orientation (hospital policies, badge, parking, EHR access) followed by 3–5 days of unit orientation. ICU travelers typically get more unit orientation time than med-surg. You will not know the hospital's culture, unwritten rules, or where anything is — that's expected and no one will judge you for asking.

First day strategy: Arrive early. Bring a notepad. Write down where supplies are, who the charge nurses are, and the unit's most important workflows. Be humble, be helpful, and don't compare this hospital to your last one out loud — even if it's worse.

Navigating a New Hospital

Every hospital's EHR is slightly different (Epic is Epic, but custom build-outs vary wildly). Ask for an EHR tip sheet on day one. For medication administration, locate the Pyxis and confirm the override list for your unit. For emergencies, know where the crash cart is before you need it — check this on your first shift, not your fifth.

Financial Setup for Your First Assignment

Open a separate checking account specifically for travel stipend income — this simplifies tax documentation enormously. Keep receipts for all housing expenses (tax home documentation). Track your per diem actual spend vs. stipend — the surplus is yours.

Common First-Assignment Mistakes

  • Accepting overtime on week 1 before you know the unit workflow
  • Not reading your contract thoroughly before signing (especially cancellation clauses)
  • Moving communication with your recruiter off-platform before the contract is signed
  • Complaining about the hospital to staff nurses — they're permanent staff there
  • Not documenting your tax home expenses from day one
