CRNA Programs in California 2026

Part of the CRNA Career Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

California has relatively few CRNA programs for its population, which makes them intensely competitive — but it also has one of the highest CRNA pay scales in the country. Here's the California landscape and how an ICU nurse in Los Angeles, the Bay Area, or San Diego competes.

California CRNA market context: California pays nurse anesthetists more than almost anywhere — new graduates frequently start at $220,000–$260,000, and experienced CRNAs in some markets exceed $300,000. But the state has only a handful of programs relative to its size, so acceptance is extremely competitive and many California nurses also apply out of state.

All California programs are doctoral-entry following the Council on Accreditation's January 1, 2025 phase-out of master's-level entry, and all are accredited by the Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Educational Programs (COA). Confirm current tuition and start dates directly with each program — California costs vary widely between the public-partnership and private routes.

Kaiser Permanente / CSU Fullerton — Southern California

The Kaiser Permanente School of Anesthesia, offered in partnership with California State University, Fullerton, has trained nurse anesthetists since 1972 and is one of the most established programs on the West Coast. The Kaiser clinical network gives students large, integrated case volume across Kaiser's Southern California facilities. For California nurses already in the Kaiser system, this program is a natural target.

USC Keck — Los Angeles

USC's Program of Nurse Anesthesia is a full-time, 36-month hybrid program blending in-person instruction, high-fidelity simulation, and remote didactic learning, with clinical rotations at leading hospitals across greater Los Angeles. The program is accredited through Spring 2033. The USC brand and the depth of LA-area clinical sites make it a flagship Southern California option — and a highly competitive one.

Loma Linda, Samuel Merritt, and National University

Loma Linda University runs a competitive program that admits a small cohort (historically up to around 18 students) and runs roughly 39 months, front-loading didactic coursework before clinical immersion. Samuel Merritt University in Oakland serves the Bay Area, and National University serves the San Diego region. Small cohorts across the board mean California acceptance is among the most competitive in the country — strong applicants routinely apply to several California programs plus out-of-state schools.

California programs want sustained adult critical care — ventilated patients on vasoactive infusions with arterial and central lines. CVICU and trauma SICU roles at these academic systems produce the strongest applications. With so few seats, the quality and acuity of your ICU experience carries extra weight in California.

The Application Timeline from California

Because California program calendars differ, verify each school's deadlines individually and plan to apply 10–14 months ahead of your target start. Given the scarcity of seats, building a list that includes both California programs and strong out-of-state options (Arizona, Nevada, Oregon, Texas) is a practical hedge. Have your CCRN completed and your ICU hours documented well before applications open.

Few seats, high stakes: California's program-to-population ratio means acceptance rates at individual programs can be in the single digits. Do not plan around a single California school. Applying broadly — including out of state — is the single biggest lever most California applicants have. The cost of a few extra applications is trivial against the cost of waiting a full year to reapply.
California CRNA salary on graduation: California is consistently at or near the top of the national CRNA pay scale, with new graduates often starting at $220,000–$260,000 and experienced providers in some markets exceeding $300,000. The high cost of living offsets some of that, but in raw compensation terms, California CRNA salaries are among the best in the nation. See the CRNA salary by state guide to compare markets.
