Casper Test for CRNA School 2026 — A Straightforward Prep Guide

Part of the CRNA Career Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Some CRNA and graduate health programs now ask applicants to take Casper, a situational judgment test that scores how you reason through interpersonal and ethical scenarios. It's not a knowledge exam — and preparing for it is different from studying pharmacology. Here's what to expect.

The short version: Casper is an online situational judgment test. It presents realistic scenarios and asks how you'd respond, measuring qualities like ethics, empathy, professionalism, and communication — the "non-cognitive" traits grades and test scores don't capture. You can't memorize answers, but you can prepare your reasoning and your typing speed.

What Casper measures

Casper is built to assess professional and interpersonal judgment rather than clinical facts. Scenarios might involve a workplace conflict, an ethical dilemma, or a hypothetical personal situation, and your job is to reason through what you'd do and why. Programs use it as one more data point on whether an applicant will handle the human side of a demanding clinical career well — something an ICU nurse applying to anesthesia school is often well-positioned for.

The format

The test presents a series of scenarios — typically delivered by video or text — followed by questions you answer under a time limit. Some sections are typed responses; newer versions may include short spoken responses. Because it's timed, being able to organize a clear answer quickly matters as much as the content of your answer. The exact structure evolves, so read the current official format before test day.

How it's scored

Responses are reviewed and scored, and results are sent to the programs you designate. There's no single "pass" line published to applicants — programs weigh Casper alongside grades, experience, references, and interviews. Treat it as a real component that can help or hurt at the margins, not as a formality.

How to prepare (ethically)

You can't and shouldn't try to memorize model answers — the point is authentic judgment. But you can prepare productively:

Practice the format. Do timed practice scenarios so the clock doesn't rattle you and your typing keeps up with your thinking. Build a reasoning framework. For any dilemma, consider the perspectives involved, the relevant professional and ethical principles, the options, and the consequences — then commit to a defensible course. Lean on your nursing experience. As an ICU nurse you navigate ethics, family conflict, and teamwork constantly; draw on that real judgment rather than inventing an idealized voice. Communicate clearly. Structure beats eloquence — state your position, your reasoning, and your consideration of other views.

Logistics matter. Casper has technical requirements — a reliable computer, webcam, microphone, and internet. Test your setup ahead of time, find a quiet space, and register early enough that your scores reach programs before their deadlines.

Where it fits in your application

Casper is one piece of a whole. It won't overcome weak ICU experience or a thin personal statement, and a strong Casper won't carry an otherwise incomplete file. Prepare for it seriously but proportionally, and keep your energy on the load-bearing parts of the application — experience, CCRN, references, and essays.

Bottom line: If a target program requires Casper, treat it as a genuine assessment of judgment you already exercise at the bedside. Practice the format, build a clear reasoning approach, sort your tech early, and let your real clinical maturity show. Confirm current format and requirements on the official test site before you register.

General information only. Test format, requirements, and which programs require Casper change over time — verify current details on the official Casper/Acuity Insights site and with each program.

Frequently asked questions

Can you study for Casper?
Not by memorizing answers — it measures authentic judgment. But you can practice the timed format and build a clear reasoning framework for scenarios.

Is Casper a pass/fail test?
There's no published pass line for applicants. Programs weigh it alongside grades, experience, references, and interviews.

What do I need technically?
A reliable computer, webcam, microphone, and internet. Test your setup and register early enough for scores to reach programs before deadlines.
