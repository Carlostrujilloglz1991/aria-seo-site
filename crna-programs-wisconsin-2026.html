CRNA Programs in Wisconsin 2026

Wisconsin spreads its nurse anesthesia training across Milwaukee, Madison, Oshkosh, and La Crosse, with Marquette as the best-known anchor. The state's academic Level I trauma centers give ICU nurses strong clinical ground for a competitive CRNA application. Here's how the Wisconsin programs compare.

Wisconsin CRNA market context: Wisconsin's programs are tightly tied to high-volume academic systems in Milwaukee and Madison and to the Mayo Clinic Health System in the west. Reported acceptance rates run low (mid-teens), so a strong, high-acuity ICU background matters. New graduates in Wisconsin commonly start around $185,000–$215,000.

Confirm current accreditation status, tuition, credit counts, and clinical-hour requirements directly with each school.

Marquette University

Marquette University's College of Nursing in Milwaukee offers a COA-accredited DNP nurse anesthesia program spanning roughly 36 months of full-time study. It uses a small cohort model — about 14 students admitted each year — and draws on Milwaukee's high-acuity academic and cardiac centers for clinical placements. As the most established and recognized program in the state, Marquette is the anchor for many Wisconsin applicants. The small cohort makes admission highly competitive.

Edgewood, UW-Oshkosh, and the UW-La Crosse Pathway

Edgewood University in Madison and the University of Wisconsin–Oshkosh round out the state's options with COA-accredited DNP programs anchoring the Madison and Fox Valley regions. In western Wisconsin, the University of Wisconsin–La Crosse participates in an accredited nurse anesthesia pathway in partnership with the Mayo Clinic Health System School of Anesthesia and Viterbo University, where students complete a three-year curriculum culminating in a DNP — a distinctive option tied to the Mayo clinical system. Together these programs make it realistic for a Wisconsin ICU nurse to train within the state.

Programs want sustained adult critical care managing ventilated patients on vasoactive infusions with arterial and central lines. A CVICU or trauma SICU role at one of these systems is far stronger on a CRNA application than a step-down or community "ICU" that does not manage that acuity.

The Application Timeline from Wisconsin

Working backward from a fall 2027 start: most Wisconsin programs accept applications roughly September–December 2026, interview January–March 2027, and decide by spring 2027. Have your CCRN completed and at least one year (ideally two) of high-acuity ICU hours documented before applications open. With programs across Milwaukee, Madison, Oshkosh, and La Crosse — plus easy reach into Illinois and Minnesota — Wisconsin applicants can build a strong list without relocating during school.

Small cohorts make admission tight: Marquette admits roughly 14 students a year, and the other Wisconsin programs are similarly small with low acceptance rates. Don't apply only to Marquette — pair it with Edgewood, UW-Oshkosh, the La Crosse/Mayo pathway, and one or two out-of-state programs to widen your odds.
Wisconsin CRNA salary on graduation: New CRNAs in Wisconsin commonly earn an estimated $185,000–$215,000 in hospital employment, with rural and locum assignments pushing higher. Milwaukee and Madison both carry steady surgical volume and anesthesia demand.
