Trusted Health Review 2026

Part of the Travel Nursing Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Trusted Health is the travel nurse agency built like a tech company: you browse jobs with real pay listed, manage your credentials in one digital profile, and never field a cold call from a commission-hungry recruiter. Here's how the model actually works — and where it falls short.

The one-line version: Trusted Health replaces the traditional recruiter with a self-serve job marketplace plus a salaried "Care Team." You get transparency and control; you give up the aggressive advocate who hustles to squeeze extra dollars out of a facility on your behalf.

How the platform model works

With a legacy agency, the workflow is recruiter-centric: you hand your details to a person, they pitch you jobs, and the pay package often shifts depending on who you talk to and how hard you push. Trusted Health flips this. You build one digital profile — licenses, certifications, skills checklists, references — and then browse open contracts yourself in the app, with the pay package displayed before you ever talk to a human. When you apply, the profile submits electronically, which routinely shaves days off submission time compared to agencies that assemble packets manually.

Instead of one recruiter who owns your file, you get a Care Team: salaried clinical and support staff who handle questions, compliance, and problems during a contract. Because they aren't paid commission per placement, there's no incentive to pressure you into a marginal contract before the end of the month.

Pay transparency: the real headline feature

The single biggest reason nurses switch to Trusted is seeing the actual weekly gross — taxable hourly rate plus stipends — on the job listing itself. In the traditional model, the bill rate is hidden and the recruiter decides how much of it reaches you, which is exactly how two nurses on the same unit end up $300/week apart. Listing pay up front doesn't guarantee Trusted's number is the market's highest, but it kills the information asymmetry that recruiters have historically exploited.

Transparency is not the same as maximum pay. A skilled negotiator working a relationship-heavy agency can sometimes beat a posted Trusted package on an identical contract, because some agencies hold margin back to "find" during negotiation. If you enjoy negotiating and have a strong recruiter, comparison-shop every contract. If you don't, Trusted's posted number spares you the game entirely.

Benefits and the fine print

Trusted runs a fairly standard W-2 travel package: major medical coverage that can start at the beginning of a contract, dental and vision options, a 401(k), licensure and certification reimbursement on qualifying contracts, and standard travel reimbursements. Where nurses report friction is between contracts — as with most agencies, keeping benefits active during gaps has rules and time limits, so read the continuation policy before you plan a month off. Guaranteed-hours language, cancellation policy, and missed-shift penalties live in the contract itself; the app makes the document easy to read, but you still have to actually read it.

Where the model shows its limits

Job volume ebbs with the market: in tight quarters, big legacy agencies with deep exclusive facility relationships sometimes show contracts Trusted doesn't have, particularly in smaller markets and niche specialties. And the self-serve model assumes you know what you're doing — a first-time traveler who doesn't yet understand tax homes, stipend rules, or how to vet a facility gets less hand-holding than a good recruiter would provide. If that's you, read our travel nurse tax guide and housing stipend guide before your first contract, whoever you sign with.

Who it fits — and who it doesn't

Trusted fits experienced travelers who know their worth, want the real number up front, and prefer managing their career from an app instead of a phone log. It also suits nurses burned by recruiter churn — the model can't reassign "your" recruiter, because there isn't one. It fits less well for brand-new travelers who want coaching, nurses in niche specialties or rural markets where legacy relationships still control the job flow, and negotiators who consistently out-earn posted rates.

Bottom line: Trusted Health delivers on its core promise — transparent pay, modern credentialing, and no commission-driven pressure. Treat it as a strong default and a permanent benchmarking tool: even if you sign elsewhere, its posted packages tell you what the market actually pays. Keep profiles with one or two other agencies so job volume never bottlenecks a contract search.
