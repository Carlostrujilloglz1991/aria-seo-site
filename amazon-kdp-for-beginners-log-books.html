Amazon KDP Log Books: How to Publish Your First One (2026 Guide)

Written by a KDP publisher | June 2026 | 10-minute read

Log books are the quietest corner of Amazon KDP — and one of the most consistently profitable.

Unlike novels, you don't need writing ability. Unlike workbooks, you don't need subject-matter expertise. A log book is a structured template — date fields, column headers, note sections — printed on demand by Amazon and shipped to buyers who use them for years.

Here's exactly how to go from zero to published in one week.

Why Log Books Sell

Log books sell because people have ongoing tracking needs that apps don't fully serve. A nurse who logs procedures physically can take it to CRNA school interviews. A pilot who logs flight hours in a paper logbook meets FAA record-keeping requirements. A plant parent who tracks watering schedules on paper doesn't lose data when their phone breaks.

Amazon's print-on-demand model means no inventory risk. A buyer orders, Amazon prints it in a warehouse near them, ships it, and deposits 60% of the list price into your KDP account. You do nothing after the initial upload.

Royalty math: A 6×9 paperback at $9.99 earns ~$4.29 per copy after Amazon's printing cost. 10 sales/day across 50 books = $2,145/month. The books don't go out of print.

What Makes a Log Book Sell

The highest-converting log books share four features:

  1. Specific niche — "Nurse Procedure Log" outsells "Medical Log" because specificity signals the buyer is understood
  2. Pre-formatted columns — buyers want structure, not blank lines
  3. Right size — 6×9 is the standard; it fits in pockets and bags
  4. Enough pages — 100–120 pages = ~1 year of use at reasonable pace

Step-by-Step: Your First Log Book in 7 Days

Day 1: Pick Your Niche

Start with a niche you understand. The narrower and more specific, the better. "Daily Planner" has 50,000 competitors. "Travel Nurse Contract and Assignment Log" has 200.

Research on Amazon: search your niche, look at books with 10–100 reviews and #1–50,000 BSR (Best Seller Rank). That range means real sales with low enough competition to rank.

Strong log book niches in 2026: professional trade logs (electrician, plumber, HVAC), clinical specialty logs (ICU, NICU, CRNA prep), hobbyist logs (birding, fishing, amateur radio), fitness logs by method (powerlifting, marathon training, pickleball).

Day 2–3: Build the Interior

Your interior is the actual pages buyers receive. For a log book, this means:

  • A title page (book title, subtitle, "Volume 1" if you plan a series)
  • An instructions page ("How to use this log")
  • The log pages — your repeating table template
  • Optional: notes pages at the end

Tools: Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or Canva. Create a table with 5–7 columns, header row in dark background with white text, alternating row shading. Margins must be at least 0.5 inches on all sides; interior pages go to 6×9 or 8.5×11 trim size.

Export as PDF. This is your print-ready interior file.

Day 4: Create the Cover

KDP requires a full cover wrap — front cover, spine, and back cover as a single file. Cover dimensions depend on your page count.

KDP's Cover Calculator (kdp.amazon.com/cover-calculator) gives you exact pixel dimensions based on page count and trim size. Enter those into Canva or your design tool.

Cover design rules that sell:

  • Title large, readable at thumbnail size
  • Dark background with high-contrast title text
  • Professional subtitle that explains who the book is for
  • No stock photo scenes — solid color or gradient performs just as well

Export as PDF (300 DPI, CMYK or RGB both accepted by KDP).

Day 5: KDP Account Setup

Go to kdp.amazon.com and sign in with your Amazon account or create one. Complete:

  • Author/publisher name (can be a pen name)
  • Tax interview (your SSN, EIN, or foreign tax ID)
  • Bank account or routing information for royalty payments

The tax interview is the part most people procrastinate on. It takes 5 minutes. Do it before you publish — royalties are withheld until it's complete.

Day 6: Upload and Configure

In KDP, click "+ Paperback". Fill in:

  • Title — include your main keyword naturally
  • Subtitle — expand with secondary keywords
  • Author — your pen name or real name
  • Description — 2–4 paragraphs explaining who the book is for and what it does
  • Keywords — 7 keyword phrases; think about what buyers search, not what describes the book
  • Categories — pick 2; BISAC codes under Self-Help, Medical Reference, or Sports/Recreation work well for logs
  • Pricing — $7.99–$13.99 is the sweet spot; go below $7.99 and royalties barely cover printing

Upload your interior PDF and cover PDF. KDP's previewer will show how it looks printed. Review every page before submitting.

Day 7: Submit and Wait

Hit Publish. KDP reviews take 24–72 hours. If rejected, they'll tell you why — usually a margin issue or image resolution problem. Fix and resubmit.

Once live, your book appears on Amazon.com, .co.uk, .de, .co.jp, and other Amazon marketplaces automatically.

Scaling: From 1 Book to 100

The first book teaches you the system. Books 2–100 are faster because the interior template is reusable — change the column headers and title, and you have a new book for a new niche.

What Most Beginners Get Wrong

Too-broad niche: "Daily planner" is 50,000 books. "Wildland firefighter incident log" is 10. The second one gets found. Wide niches are won by publishers with 10,000+ reviews and $5,000/month ad budgets.

Under-priced: At $4.99, your royalty on a 6×9 paperback is $0.66 after printing. You need 75 sales to earn what 10 sales at $9.99 earn. Price at $8.99–$12.99.

No description: The description is sales copy. "This log book helps you track your shifts" is not sales copy. "Finally, a log built specifically for travel nurses — compact enough to fit in your scrubs, structured for how you actually document" is.

Waiting for perfect: The first book you publish will be your worst. Publish it anyway. You'll improve the template on book 2, and book 1 keeps selling in the background.

The honest timeline: Your first book earns almost nothing. Your tenth book earns a little. Your fiftieth book, combined with the other 49, earns meaningfully. KDP is a portfolio play — the income compounds with each new title. Start now so the compounding clock starts sooner.
