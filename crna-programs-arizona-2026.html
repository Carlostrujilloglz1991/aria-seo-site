Arizona ICU nurses pursuing CRNA have strong local options plus a compelling case for applying out-of-state. Here's what the Arizona programs look like and how to build a competitive application from a Phoenix or Tucson hospital.

Arizona CRNA market context: Arizona is a high-demand CRNA market. Phoenix metro and Tucson have significant hospital growth, an aging population, and ongoing nurse anesthetist shortages. Graduates of Arizona CRNA programs who stay in-state enter a favorable job market with starting salaries of $195,000–$230,000.

Midwestern University — Glendale

Midwestern University's nurse anesthesia program is one of the more established programs in the Southwest. The program is full-time, 36 months, and based at their Glendale campus. Clinical rotations are placed at partner hospitals throughout the Phoenix metro and occasionally out of state for subspecialty experience.

Minimum requirements as of 2026: current RN license, BSN from accredited program, minimum 3.0 GPA (science GPA weighted), 1 year ICU experience (competitive applicants average 2–3 years), CCRN preferred, GRE not required. The program accepts approximately 20–25 students per cohort. Application typically opens in late spring/early summer for the following year's class.

The private school tuition is higher than the U of A option, but the Phoenix clinical placement network is well-established and the program has strong NBCRNA first-time pass rates.

University of Arizona — Tucson

The University of Arizona offers in-state tuition for Arizona residents, making it the more cost-effective option for Arizona nurses. The program is housed in the College of Nursing and has clinical affiliations with Banner Health, Tucson Medical Center, and other regional systems. The program is 36 months, DNP-A.

In-state tuition of $60,000–$80,000 total versus $95,000–$110,000 at Midwestern represents a $15,000–$50,000 difference — meaningful when both programs produce Arizona-licensed CRNAs. Competitive applicants average 2–3 years ICU experience and most have CCRN. The Tucson campus draws applicants from both Tucson and Phoenix; being Phoenix-based doesn't disqualify you.

The Application Timeline from Arizona

Working backward from a fall 2027 program start: application submission is typically September–December 2026 for most programs. Interview invitations go out January–March 2027. Acceptance decisions by April–May 2027. You should have your CCRN completed and your ICU hours documented before the application opens.

Arizona ICU nurses applying to out-of-state programs as well as local ones significantly improve their odds. Programs with strong reputations and Arizona clinical affiliation opportunities include Baylor (TX), Texas Wesleyan (TX), and programs in Nevada and Colorado — all accessible from Arizona without cross-country relocation during school.

The ICU type matters in Arizona: Some Arizona ICU positions — particularly in smaller community hospitals — may not satisfy CRNA program requirements for "critical care experience." Before accepting an ICU position specifically to build CRNA credentials, confirm the unit manages ventilated patients requiring vasoactive infusions, arterial lines, and invasive monitoring. A progressive care or step-down unit labeled "ICU" at a smaller facility may not qualify.
Arizona CRNA salary on graduation: New CRNA graduates in Phoenix metro are earning $195,000–$225,000 in hospital employment, with independent contractor and locums opportunities reaching $250,000–$300,000+. Arizona has independent practice authority for CRNAs, meaning you can practice without mandatory physician oversight — expanding your practice opportunities significantly compared to supervision-requirement states.
