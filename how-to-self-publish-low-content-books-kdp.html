How to Self-Publish Low-Content Books on Amazon KDP in 2026 (Real Guide)

Last updated: June 2026 | Reading time: 9 minutes

Low-content books — journals, planners, logbooks, coloring books, trackers — are one of the most accessible passive income streams that exist. You create the file once, upload it to Amazon KDP, and collect royalties indefinitely. No inventory. No shipping. No customer service. Amazon handles everything.

I have published 52 of these books. Here is what I know.

What is a low-content book? A book with minimal written content — mostly structure, prompts, blank pages, or templates. Journals, fitness trackers, habit logs, recipe books, password keepers, coloring books. The reader fills it in. You supply the framework.

What Makes a Low-Content Book Sell

Most people who try KDP fail because they publish generic books. "Daily Journal" with lined pages. A gratitude journal with no niche. A planner with no target audience.

The books that actually sell are specific:

  • "Gratitude Journal for Men" outsells "Gratitude Journal" 10:1
  • "Blood Pressure Log Book for Seniors" outsells "Health Tracker"
  • "BSN-to-CRNA Roadmap Journal" outsells "Nursing Career Journal"

Specificity signals the right buyer. Generic books disappear in search results. Specific books appear when the exact right person is searching.

Step 1: Choose a Niche

Search Amazon for your niche. Look at the BSR (Best Seller Rank) on the books that appear. A book with a BSR under 100,000 is selling at least a few copies per month. A book under 50,000 is selling consistently.

Look for niches where:

  • The top 5 results have BSRs under 100,000 (proof the demand exists)
  • Most books are 3 years old or older (there's room for a fresh, better-designed option)
  • The reviews mention what the current books are missing (that's your differentiation)

Step 2: Create the Interior

The interior is the inside pages. For a journal, this is lined or dotted pages with prompts. For a tracker, this is structured tables and fill-in sections.

Tools:

  • Canva (free tier works) — drag-and-drop, then export to PDF
  • Microsoft Word — simple but functional
  • Google Docs — free, export as PDF
  • Python + ReportLab — what I use for all 52 books; generates PDFs programmatically

KDP requires PDF format. The most common trim sizes: 5x8 (small journal), 6x9 (standard journal), 8.5x11 (planner or workbook).

Step 3: Create the Cover

Your cover is your marketing. It's the only thing a buyer sees before clicking. It needs to:

  • Clearly communicate what the book is
  • Look professional at thumbnail size (it will be shown at about 100x150 pixels)
  • Include the title in large, readable font

KDP provides a Cover Creator tool for free. Canva has KDP cover templates. For print books, KDP provides a cover template calculator — use it, because spine width changes with page count.

Step 4: Set Up Your KDP Account

  1. Go to kdp.amazon.com and create an account
  2. Complete your tax information (W-9 for US residents, W-8 for international) — this is required before Amazon pays you
  3. Set up your payment method (direct deposit recommended)
  4. Click "Create New Title" and follow the upload flow
The W-9 is the most important first step. Amazon holds all royalties until your tax information is on file. Don't publish 50 books and then forget this — you'll have 50 books earning money you can't access.

Step 5: Set Your Price and Keywords

Pricing for low-content books:

  • Small journals (5x8, under 150 pages): $7.99–$11.99
  • Standard journals (6x9, 150+ pages): $10.99–$14.99
  • Large planners (8.5x11, 200+ pages): $11.99–$16.99

KDP gives you 7 keyword fields. Use all 7. Search Amazon yourself for your book — the autocomplete suggestions are real search terms people use.

The Etsy Play

Once you have KDP books, you have the PDF files. Those same files can be sold on Etsy as "printable PDFs" — instant digital downloads. Etsy's audience skews female, craft-adjacent, and gift-buying. It's a different buyer than Amazon. Your margins on Etsy are higher (no printing cost) but volume is lower per listing.

Running KDP + Etsy from the same content library is the closest thing I've found to genuinely passive income from a single piece of work.
