The University of Iowa's College of Nursing runs the only nurse anesthesia program in the state. It awards a Doctor of Nursing Practice in Nurse Anesthesia, takes 36 months to complete full-time, and is accredited by both the Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Educational Programs (COA) and the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education (CCNE). The program is designed for registered nurses who already hold at least a bachelor's degree in nursing.

Why the Class Is So Small

The University of Iowa admits only about 16 students per cohort, and that figure is deliberate. Iowa has a limited number of high-volume clinical anesthesia sites, so a small class size is necessary to ensure every student gets enough hands-on cases to graduate competent and certification-ready. For applicants, the trade-off is clear: the clinical experience is well-protected, but the seats are scarce. That scarcity is the single most important fact in planning an Iowa application.

Admission Requirements

The Iowa program follows the national CRNA template. You will need a BSN (or equivalent bachelor's in nursing) with a competitive GPA, a minimum of one year of adult critical-care experience, and current BLS, ACLS, and PALS certifications. As everywhere, the ICU requirement is non-negotiable, and competitive applicants typically bring more than the minimum year, high-acuity experience, and the CCRN credential — see the CCRN guide. For the full checklist, review the general CRNA school requirements.

Sixteen seats means you must apply widely. With one program and a small cohort, treating Iowa as your only target is a mistake. Build a regional list that includes nearby states with established programs — Minnesota, Wisconsin, Missouri, and Nebraska are all within reasonable reach and expand your odds considerably.
Confirm the current numbers. Cohort size, tuition, program length, and admission rules change year to year. Treat the figures here as a planning baseline and verify the University of Iowa's current details on its official site and the COA accredited-programs list before applying.

Application Strategy for Iowa

Because Iowa has a single small program, your plan has to be regional from day one. Apply to the University of Iowa, but build a list that also includes Minnesota, Wisconsin, Missouri, and Nebraska to give yourself realistic odds across a competitive applicant pool. Strengthen your file with the required ICU year (ideally more), the CCRN, and a sharp personal statement. For realistic expectations, see CRNA acceptance rates.

Bottom Line

The University of Iowa in Iowa City is the state's only accredited CRNA program: a 36-month, dual-accredited DNP in Nurse Anesthesia that admits roughly 16 students a year to protect clinical quality. It's an excellent program, but with one small cohort, Iowa applicants must apply regionally to give themselves a fair shot. For the bigger picture, see how to become a CRNA and the CRNA job outlook.
