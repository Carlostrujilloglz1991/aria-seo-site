How Much Do ICU Nurses Make by State 2026

Financial Disclaimer: Salary figures below are based on BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics, facility-reported data, and industry surveys. Actual pay varies by employer, shift, certifications, and years of experience.

ICU nurses earn meaningfully more than general floor nurses — typically 10 to 20 percent above the RN median in their state — due to patient acuity, required certifications, and mandatory overtime in understaffed units. But geography is the single largest variable in an ICU nurse's paycheck, with the highest-paying states outpacing the lowest by nearly two to one.

National median: ICU and critical care RNs earn approximately $85,000 to $95,000 annually in base pay as of 2026. With night shift differentials, charge nurse pay, CCRN certification bonuses, and overtime, total compensation frequently reaches $100,000 to $130,000+ in high-cost states.

Lowest-Paying States for ICU Nurses

ICU nurses in the South and Midwest typically earn base salaries in the $65,000 to $78,000 range. States including Mississippi, Alabama, West Virginia, Arkansas, and South Dakota consistently rank at the bottom of RN pay scales. However, cost of living in these states is substantially lower, and the effective purchasing power of an ICU nurse's salary in rural Mississippi versus urban California can be surprisingly comparable once rent, taxes, and living costs are factored in.

What Shifts the Number Most

Night shift differential

Most ICU units pay a night shift differential of $4 to $8 per hour — sometimes higher at Level I trauma centers and academic medical centers. A nurse working three 12-hour night shifts per week earns roughly $7,500 to $15,000 more per year than a pure day-shift nurse at the same base rate.

CCRN and specialty certifications

CCRN certification typically adds $2,000 to $6,000 in annual pay through certification bonuses, hourly pay bumps, or both. Specialty certifications like CCRN-K, PCCN, and CMC (cardiac medicine) may add smaller but still meaningful amounts. The ROI on CCRN is very high — the exam fee is a few hundred dollars, and the pay increase is permanent at many facilities.

Charge nurse pay

Charge RN premium typically ranges from $2 to $5 per hour above base. For an ICU nurse picking up four charge shifts per week, this adds $5,000 to $12,000 annually on top of base pay.

Overtime

Federal law requires overtime at 1.5x for hours over 40 per week. For an ICU nurse at $42 per hour base, overtime rate is $63 per hour. A nurse working two extra 12-hour shifts per month generates roughly $18,000 in gross overtime annually — before tax.

Travel ICU Nurse Pay vs. Staff Pay

Travel ICU nurses consistently earn 20 to 40 percent more in total compensation than staff nurses in the same unit. The gap is most dramatic in high-cost states like California, where crisis travel contracts in ICU units have paid $80 to $100+ per hour in recent years.
