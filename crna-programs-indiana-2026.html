CRNA Programs in Indiana 2026

Indiana's nurse anesthesia training centers on Indianapolis, with additional options in Evansville and Terre Haute. The state's Level I trauma centers and large academic systems give ICU nurses strong ground from which to build a competitive CRNA application. Here's how the Indiana programs compare.

Indiana CRNA market context: Indiana combines solid in-state programs with a low cost of living, making it a practical place to train. New graduates in Indiana commonly start around $175,000–$200,000, with rural and locum assignments reaching higher. Indianapolis anchors most of the state's high-acuity clinical training.

Indiana has COA-accredited nurse anesthesia programs anchored in Indianapolis, Evansville, and Terre Haute. Every accredited U.S. program now awards a doctorate — the COA phased out master's-level CRNA entry on January 1, 2025. Some programs award the DNP and others the DNAP; both qualify graduates to sit for the National Certification Examination. Always confirm current accreditation status, tuition, credit counts, and clinical-hour requirements directly with each school and on the COA website.

Marian University

Marian University's Leighton School of Nursing in Indianapolis runs a COA-accredited DNP nurse anesthesia track with its next program review scheduled for May 2032 — a long accreditation horizon that signals stability. The program admits one cohort per year (around 34 students) and benefits from Indianapolis's concentration of high-acuity hospitals. As the largest in-state program in the capital, Marian is the anchor for many Indiana applicants.

University of Evansville and Indiana State University

The University of Evansville offers a Doctor of Nurse Anesthesia Practice (DNAP) in southwestern Indiana; its curriculum meets the educational requirements for graduates to sit for the National Certifying Examination and pursue licensure in all 50 states. Indiana State University in Terre Haute rounds out the state's options with a COA-accredited DNP. Together these programs make it realistic for an Indiana ICU nurse to train close to home rather than relocating across the country.

The Application Timeline from Indiana

Working backward from a fall 2027 start: most Indiana programs accept applications roughly September–December 2026, interview January–March 2027, and decide by spring 2027. Have your CCRN completed and at least one year (ideally two) of high-acuity ICU hours documented before applications open. With programs in Indianapolis, Evansville, and Terre Haute — plus easy reach into Ohio, Illinois, and Kentucky — Indiana applicants can build a strong list without relocating during school.

Small cohorts, single annual intake: Indiana programs admit one cohort per year, so a missed cycle costs a full year. Don't apply only to Marian — pair it with Evansville, Indiana State, and one or two out-of-state programs so a single competitive cycle doesn't stall your timeline.
Indiana CRNA salary on graduation: New CRNAs in Indiana commonly earn an estimated $175,000–$200,000 in hospital employment, with rural and locum assignments pushing higher. Indianapolis and the surrounding region carry steady surgical volume and anesthesia demand.
