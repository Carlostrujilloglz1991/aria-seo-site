CRNA Programs in Wyoming

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

Wyoming has no in-state accredited CRNA program. For Wyoming nurses, the path runs through neighboring Mountain West states — and because CRNAs are vital to rural anesthesia care, a Wyoming background is an asset on those applications.

The landscape: There is no COA-accredited nurse anesthesia program located in Wyoming. CRNAs practice across the state — often as the primary anesthesia providers in rural hospitals — but training happens elsewhere. Wyoming applicants should plan to relocate to a nearby state for school.

Why Wyoming Has No Program (and Why That's Manageable)

Wyoming is the least-populous state, with relatively few high-volume surgical centers — not enough concentrated case volume to anchor a nurse anesthesia program. The upside is that CRNAs are especially valued in Wyoming's rural and frontier hospitals, so an applicant who frames a clear plan to train regionally and return to practice in Wyoming tells a compelling story.

Admission Requirements

Wherever you apply, the national CRNA template holds. You will need a BSN (or equivalent qualifying nursing degree) with a competitive GPA, a minimum of one year of full-time adult critical-care experience, an unencumbered RN license, and current BLS and ACLS certification. The ICU requirement is non-negotiable, and competitive applicants typically bring more than the minimum — the CCRN credential strengthens a file; see the CCRN guide. For the complete checklist, review the general CRNA school requirements.

Make your rural-practice intent part of the story. Programs in the Mountain West value applicants committed to rural anesthesia. If you plan to return to Wyoming to practice, say so — it aligns with the clinical mission of many regional programs and strengthens your file.
Confirm the current details. Program availability, residency rules, and admission requirements change. Treat this page as a planning baseline and verify each program's current information on its official site and the COA accredited-programs list before applying.

Application Strategy for Wyoming

Plan to relocate and apply regionally from day one. Anchor your list with the closest options in Utah and Colorado, then widen it to the Pacific Northwest. Strengthen your file with the required ICU year (ideally more), the CCRN, and a sharp personal statement, and budget for relocation. For realistic expectations, see CRNA acceptance rates.

Bottom Line

Wyoming has no in-state CRNA program, so the route runs through the Mountain West — Utah and Colorado first, then the broader region. CRNAs are essential to Wyoming's rural hospitals, so a clear plan to train regionally and return home is a genuine strength.
