CRNA Programs in Virginia 2026

Medical Disclaimer: This article is general educational information for licensed clinicians and students, not medical advice or a substitute for your institution's protocols or a provider's orders. Always follow your facility's policies and a provider's orders.

Virginia is anchored by one of the oldest and most established nurse anesthesia programs in the country, plus a strong coastal option in Hampton Roads. Here's how the Virginia programs compare and how an ICU nurse in Richmond, Norfolk, or Northern Virginia builds a competitive application.

Virginia CRNA market context: Virginia blends major academic medical centers with a large military and federal healthcare footprint, creating steady anesthesia demand across the state. New graduates commonly start around $185,000–$215,000, with locum and rural assignments reaching $240,000–$290,000+. Northern Virginia's proximity to the D.C. metro adds a high-cost, high-pay submarket for experienced CRNAs.

With only two in-state programs, most competitive Virginia applicants also apply to programs in nearby North Carolina, Tennessee, Maryland, and Pennsylvania.

Virginia Commonwealth University — Richmond

VCU's Department of Nurse Anesthesia is one of the most established in the nation, with a long history and a strong national reputation. The program is based at VCU Medical Center, a Level I trauma center in downtown Richmond that gives students extraordinary case volume and acuity. VCU's DNAP is well known to recruiters across the mid-Atlantic, and its size and academic resources make it the flagship Virginia option. Admission is competitive — strong CCRN credentials and high-acuity ICU experience are effectively expected.

Old Dominion University — Norfolk / Virginia Beach

ODU's DNP nurse anesthesia program serves the Hampton Roads region, home to a dense cluster of hospitals and one of the largest military medical communities in the country. Clinical placements draw on Sentara's regional health system and other Tidewater sites. For nurses already living in southeastern Virginia, ODU offers a route to CRNA without relocating, with public-university tuition that is competitive for state residents.

The Application Timeline from Virginia

Working backward from a fall 2027 start: Virginia programs generally accept applications in the fall 2026 window, interview over the winter, and decide by spring 2027. Have your CCRN completed and ICU hours documented before applications open. With only two in-state options, apply to both plus two or three out-of-state programs so a single cycle does not hinge on one decision.

Relocation and clinical-site reality: Virginia's two programs sit at opposite ends of the state — Richmond and the Norfolk/Virginia Beach area. If you live in Northern Virginia, neither is local, and you may relocate for didactic coursework or commute for clinicals. Ask each program where its rotation sites are, whether you can rank preferences, and what housing support exists before committing.
Virginia CRNA salary on graduation: New CRNAs in Virginia commonly earn $185,000–$215,000 in hospital employment, and independent or locum work can push total compensation above $240,000–$290,000. The state's academic centers, military facilities, and the high-cost Northern Virginia submarket make it a strong place to graduate and find work.
