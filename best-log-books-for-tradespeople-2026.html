What Every Trade Log Book Should Include

Regardless of trade, a professional log book worth buying will have: date-stamped entry fields (critical for licensing boards), a clear field for supervisor or inspector names, enough space per entry for meaningful notes (not just a check box), and a durable cover that survives a truck toolbox or job site bag.

What to avoid: generic "job diary" notebooks with no trade-specific structure, undated formats (licensing boards need dates), and anything that requires you to create your own columns — that friction means it won't get used consistently.

Do I Need a Trade-Specific Log Book or Will Any Notebook Work?

For casual notes, any notebook works. For OJT hour documentation submitted to a licensing board, trade-specific format matters. Most state electrical, plumbing, and HVAC licensing boards specify what information must appear in hour logs. A generic notebook often misses required fields — and missing fields can get your application sent back, delaying your license by months.

If your state requires specific documentation format, verify with your licensing board before your final year of apprenticeship. The log books above are formatted to meet common requirements but state regulations vary.
