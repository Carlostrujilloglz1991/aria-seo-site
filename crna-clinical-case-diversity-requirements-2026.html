CRNA Clinical Case Diversity Requirements 2026

The Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Programs (COA) sets case diversity standards. Programs that don't meet them risk accreditation issues — and SRNAs who don't complete required cases cannot graduate.

COA Case Diversity Standards (Current)

COA requires programs to ensure SRNAs gain experience across multiple patient populations and anesthetic techniques. While specific numbers are updated periodically (always verify at coacrna.org), the required categories include:

  • Patients across the lifespan: neonates, infants, children, adolescents, adults, geriatrics
  • Multiple ASA physical status classes (I through IV)
  • Emergency cases
  • Obstetric anesthesia (labor epidurals, C-section)
  • Regional anesthesia techniques (neuraxial and peripheral nerve blocks)
  • Airway management techniques (direct laryngoscopy, video laryngoscopy, LMA, fiberoptic)
  • Multiple anesthetic techniques: TIVA, balanced, inhalation, regional/MAC
  • Invasive monitoring placement and management

What to Do if Your Program Lacks Case Types

Some programs have limited pediatric or OB exposure due to their clinical site affiliations. Steps if you're approaching graduation without required case categories:

  1. Flag the gap early (at the 75% completion point, not the final month)
  2. Talk to your clinical coordinator — programs can arrange supplemental rotations at affiliate sites
  3. Request elective rotations at sites with the needed case types
  4. Document any cases where you observed but did not perform — some programs count observed cases toward diversity requirements in specific categories
SRNA advocacy: You are responsible for tracking your own case log. Do not assume your program is tracking for you. Review your Typhon dashboard monthly and flag gaps proactively to your clinical coordinator.

How Case Diversity Affects NCE Performance

SRNAs with broad case diversity consistently outperform peers with narrow case exposure on NCE domain areas. The exam tests both knowledge and application — having actually managed a pediatric case, an OB epidural, or a one-lung ventilation case gives you experiential context that purely book-based preparation cannot replicate.
