What Is eBay Arbitrage?

Arbitrage means buying something in one place and selling it at a higher price in another. eBay-to-eBay arbitrage works because:

  • Sellers list items without researching comparable sold prices
  • Some sellers need quick cash and underprice
  • Listings with bad photos get fewer bids
  • Local pickup-only listings are underpriced because fewer people can buy them

Your edge: research. You know what it's worth. They listed it without checking.

How to Find Deals: The 5-Minute Research Process

Step 1: Search eBay for your category (e.g., "Lego 75192 Millennium Falcon")

Step 2: On the left sidebar, check "Sold Items" filter. This shows what the item ACTUALLY sold for in the last 90 days. This is your target price.

Step 3: Look at active listings. Find any priced 30%+ below the average sold price.

Step 4: Calculate: Sold price − eBay 13.25% fee − shipping − your cost = profit. Need $15+ minimum, ideally $30+.

Step 5: Buy it. Relist with better photos and accurate title.

Fees to Know

eBay final value fee: 13.25% of total sale price including shipping. This is the main fee. Budget it into every deal before you buy.

PayPal/payment processing: Already included in eBay's fee as of 2024.

Shipping: Use eBay's shipping calculator for an estimate. USPS Priority Mail is best for most items under 15 lbs. Get a postal scale ($15 at Walmart).

Returns: Accept returns to attract more buyers. Budget 3–5% of sales for occasional returns.
