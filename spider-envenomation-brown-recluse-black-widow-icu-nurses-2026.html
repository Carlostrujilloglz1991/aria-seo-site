Spider Envenomation: An ICU Nurse's Guide to Brown Recluse and Black Widow

Updated July 2026  |  More ICU nursing guides →

Two spiders account for nearly all medically significant envenomations in North America, and they could not be more different. The brown recluse (Loxosceles) causes a slow, local, tissue-destroying lesion — and rarely, a dangerous systemic hemolytic illness. The black widow (Latrodectus) causes almost no local wound at all but a dramatic neurotoxic syndrome of crushing muscle pain that classically mimics an acute surgical abdomen. Knowing which syndrome you are looking at changes everything about the workup, the monitoring, and what actually helps.

Scope note: Educational overview for licensed ICU and ED nurses — not a treatment protocol. Antivenom decisions, wound management, and disposition belong to the provider, toxicology, and your regional Poison Control Center
