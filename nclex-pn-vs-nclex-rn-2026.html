NCLEX-PN vs NCLEX-RN 2026: Everything You Need to Know

Both exams use Next Generation NCLEX (NGN) format as of 2023. The difference is scope of practice — not difficulty level alone.

Content Differences

The NCLEX-PN tests LPN scope: data collection (not full assessment), reinforcing teaching, carrying out orders, basic care. The NCLEX-RN tests RN scope: independent clinical judgment, care planning, delegation, complex priority-setting, and leadership.

Both use the same NGN item types (extended drag-and-drop, matrix, bow-tie, drop-down cloze), but RN case studies are more complex with more independent decision-making expected.

Which Should You Take?

Take NCLEX-PN if you completed an LPN/LVN program. Take NCLEX-RN if you completed an ADN or BSN program. You cannot substitute one for the other — the exam is tied to your program completion and state board application.

LPN bridge path: Many LPNs use their license to work while completing an LPN-to-RN bridge program, then take the NCLEX-RN. This is a smart financial move — you earn RN wages 12–18 months sooner than a direct entry student.

Study Strategy Differences

For NCLEX-PN, focus heavily on: medication administration safety, data collection vs. full assessment distinction, when to report vs. when to act within scope, basic nursing procedures, and reinforcing (not initiating) teaching plans.

For NCLEX-RN, the priority questions are harder: multi-patient triage, complex delegation chains, pharmacology rationale, and full NGN unfolding cases across all body systems.

Pass Rate Tips

Both exams respond well to the same core preparation: Uworld or ATI for question banks, prioritizing weak content areas over re-reading textbooks, and doing at least 2,000 practice questions with full rationale review before test day.
