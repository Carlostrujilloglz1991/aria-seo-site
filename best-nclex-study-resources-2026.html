How to Build a 90-Day NCLEX Study Plan

Before diving into individual reviews, here's the framework that top-passing students follow:

Weeks 1–3: Content Foundation

Use a content-heavy resource (Hurst or ATI content modules) to fill knowledge gaps. Don't start doing practice questions yet — you need to know the material first. Focus on the highest-yield systems: cardiac, respiratory, pharmacology, and maternal-newborn.

Weeks 4–10: Active Practice

Do at least 75–100 questions per day using your primary question bank (ATI or UWorld). Read every rationale — both correct and incorrect answers. This is where most of your learning actually happens. Target a 60%+ average in UWorld or 70%+ in ATI by the end of this phase.

Weeks 11–13: NGN Item Types + Weak Areas

The 2026 NCLEX-RN now includes 6 next-generation item types. Dedicate at least 2 full weeks practicing bow-tie questions, extended drag-and-drop, matrix grids, and case studies. ATI's NGN-specific practice sets are especially strong here.

Final Week: Simulated Exams + Light Review

Take 2–3 full 145-question simulated exams. Do not cram new content. Sleep 8 hours the two nights before your test date.

UWorld — Best Question Bank for High Achievers

UWorld has the best question rationales in the business. Their explanations don't just tell you why an answer is right — they include pathophysiology diagrams, nursing priority logic, and comparison tables that help you internalize concepts. The questions are notoriously harder than the actual NCLEX, which many students find confidence-building in retrospect.

✅ Pros

  • Most detailed rationales available
  • Questions harder than NCLEX = confidence buffer
  • Excellent analytics dashboard
  • Strong NGN coverage in 2025–2026 updates

❌ Cons

  • Expensive ($149–$299)
  • No built-in content review
  • Can feel overwhelming if content gaps exist

Best used with: Hurst Review or ATI content modules for the first 3 weeks, then switch to UWorld for questions only.

Kaplan NCLEX Review — Best for Test Strategy

Kaplan's biggest differentiator is its Decision Tree — a question-answering framework that helps you eliminate wrong answers even when you don't know the content. For nurses who feel confident about nursing knowledge but struggle with test-taking strategy, Kaplan can be a game-changer. However, their NGN question bank is smaller than ATI or UWorld.

✅ Pros

  • Proven Decision Tree test strategy
  • Good video lecture library
  • Money-back guarantee options

❌ Cons

  • Most expensive option ($349–$499)
  • Smaller NGN-specific question pool
  • Strategy alone won't substitute for content

Hurst Review — Best for Plugging Content Gaps

If you struggled in nursing school or feel like your content foundation is shaky, Hurst is worth every dollar. Their live and on-demand review is structured around "need to know" concepts — they ruthlessly cut low-yield topics and focus your brain on what the NCLEX actually tests. Their "M3" framework organizes pharmacology and pathophysiology in a memorable way that sticks.

✅ Pros

  • Outstanding content organization
  • M3 pharmacology framework is memorable
  • Live review option available
  • Great for learners who need structure

❌ Cons

  • Question bank is smaller and less detailed
  • Limited NGN-specific content
  • Pricey for content-review-only product

Mark Klimek — Best Free/Budget Supplement

Mark Klimek's audio and video lectures are legendary in nursing school circles. His mnemonics are entertaining, his delivery is memorable, and his lectures on delegation, prioritization, acid-base, and pharmacology are genuinely excellent. The catch: they were not designed around the NGN format and don't include a practice question bank. Use Klimek as a free supplement, not a standalone program.

✅ Pros

  • Free or very low cost
  • Memorable mnemonics
  • Excellent for delegation and prioritization

❌ Cons

  • No question bank
  • Not updated for 2026 NGN format
  • No performance analytics
