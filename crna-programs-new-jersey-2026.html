CRNA Programs in New Jersey 2026

New Jersey concentrates its nurse anesthesia training in a single, deeply established doctoral program at Rutgers in Newark — but the state's dense corridor of high-acuity hospitals between Newark, New Brunswick, and Camden gives ICU nurses an excellent base from which to build a competitive application. Here's how it works.

New Jersey CRNA market context: With one in-state program and a large population of acute-care nurses, New Jersey is a competitive admissions market — many NJ applicants also apply to programs in nearby Pennsylvania and New York. New graduates in the New York–New Jersey metro commonly start around $190,000–$220,000, among the higher regional ranges in the country.

Rutgers University DNP – Nurse Anesthesia

The Rutgers School of Nursing DNP in nurse anesthesia, based at the Newark campus, has been COA-accredited since the program's inception in 2004 and earned the maximum ten-year re-accreditation in 2018. It is a full-time, roughly 36-month doctoral track of about 85 credits and approximately 2,800 clinical hours, with the first year weighted toward didactic and foundational DNP coursework and the final two years emphasizing intensive clinical specialty training. Rutgers reports a first-time certification-exam pass rate above 90% and strong post-graduation placement. Admission typically expects a BSN with a competitive GPA (3.2+) and at least one and a half years of current, full-time critical care experience.

The Application Timeline from New Jersey

Working backward from a fall 2027 start: plan to apply to Rutgers and a handful of out-of-state programs roughly September–December 2026, interview January–March 2027, and decide by spring 2027. Have your CCRN completed and at least one year (ideally two) of high-acuity ICU hours documented before applications open. Because New Jersey sits within easy reach of Philadelphia and New York programs, NJ applicants have an unusually wide regional menu without committing to a cross-country move.

One in-state program means real competition: Rutgers draws a large regional applicant pool for a single cohort. Do not apply only in-state. Pair Rutgers with strong programs in Pennsylvania (Pittsburgh, Villanova, Drexel) and New York to widen your odds — your high-acuity NJ ICU experience travels well across state lines.
New Jersey CRNA salary on graduation: New CRNAs in the NY–NJ metro commonly earn an estimated $190,000–$220,000 in hospital employment, with locum and per-diem assignments pushing higher. Dense surgical volume across northern and central New Jersey keeps anesthesia demand steady.
