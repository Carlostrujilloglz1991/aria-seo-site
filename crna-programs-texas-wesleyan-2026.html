Texas Wesleyan CRNA Program 2026

Texas Wesleyan University runs one of the largest and best-known nurse anesthesia programs in the United States, with a distinctive front-loaded model and clinical sites spread across multiple states. Here's how it's structured and how ICU nurses build a competitive application.

Quick profile: Texas Wesleyan's Graduate Programs of Nurse Anesthesia are based in Fort Worth, Texas, and award a doctoral degree. The program is notable for its scale and its front-loaded design: didactic coursework is completed at the front end, after which residents move to clinical sites — which include locations in several states — for the clinical phase. Its size and long track record make it one of the most recognized CRNA programs in the country.

Why Texas Wesleyan

Two features define Texas Wesleyan. First, scale: it's among the largest nurse anesthesia programs in the U.S., with a large faculty, extensive alumni network, and a long institutional history in the field. Second, its distinctive structure — front-loaded didactics followed by a clinical phase distributed across a wide network of affiliated sites. That network gives residents access to varied clinical environments, and the front-loaded model concentrates the heavy classroom work before clinicals begin. For nurses drawn to an established, high-volume program with geographic breadth in its clinical placements, Texas Wesleyan is a leading option.

The Front-Loaded Structure

Texas Wesleyan's hallmark is its front-loaded model. Residents complete the didactic core — advanced physiology, pharmacology, and anesthesia principles — before entering the clinical phase, rather than blending classroom and clinicals from the start. Once the clinical phase begins, residents are placed at affiliated sites, and because the clinical network spans multiple states, placement location is an important planning consideration. Like all accredited programs since the Council on Accreditation's January 1, 2025 doctoral-entry requirement, the degree awarded is a doctorate. The program is full-time; working during it is generally not realistic.

Plan for clinical relocation: Because Texas Wesleyan's clinical sites are distributed geographically, you may complete clinicals away from Fort Worth. Factor potential relocation and housing during the clinical phase into your financial plan and life logistics before committing.

Building a Competitive Application

Strong Texas Wesleyan applicants pair solid academics with genuine critical-care depth: a year or more in a high-acuity ICU independently managing ventilated patients on vasoactive drips with arterial and central lines, the CCRN, and CRNA shadowing that lets them speak specifically about the role. Essays and interviews should convey a clear, informed reason for pursuing anesthesia. Given the program's scale and reputation, the applicant pool is strong — leadership, precepting, and high-acuity unit experience help you stand out.

Cost and Financial Planning

Texas Wesleyan is a private university, so tuition is on the higher end, and the full-time doctoral structure means little income during the program. Most residents finance through federal graduate loans and savings, and should budget for possible relocation during the clinical phase. The offset is strong on the back end: new CRNAs commonly start around $185,000–$215,000, with locum and high-demand assignments reaching $240,000–$290,000+. Model your total cost of attendance, including clinical-phase housing, before committing.

Bottom line: Texas Wesleyan is one of the largest, most established nurse anesthesia programs in the country, defined by its front-loaded model and geographically distributed clinical network. Target it with a year or more of high-acuity ICU, a CCRN, and a specific application — and plan financially for the front-loaded didactic year and possible clinical relocation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "front-loaded" mean? You complete the classroom/didactic core before starting clinicals, rather than mixing them from day one. It concentrates academic work early and clinical work later.

Will I have to move for clinicals? Possibly. Texas Wesleyan's clinical sites span multiple states, so your placement may be outside Fort Worth. Plan for that logistically and financially.

Is it a good program? Yes — it's large, established, and widely recognized. Confirm current certification pass rates and outcomes on the program's published data.
