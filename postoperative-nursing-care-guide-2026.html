PACU (Post-Anesthesia Care Unit) Assessment Priorities

When receiving a patient from the OR, perform a systematic handoff assessment BEFORE the anesthesia provider leaves. Use the SBAR format to receive information about the procedure, anesthesia type, intraoperative events, estimated blood loss, fluids given, and current orders.

Initial PACU Assessment (First 15 Minutes)

  • Airway: Patent? Snoring/stridor? Jaw-thrust needed? SpO2?
  • Breathing: Rate, depth, effort; SpO2; supplemental O2 in place?
  • Circulation: HR, rhythm, BP (compare to pre-op baseline); color, capillary refill; IV access and fluids running
  • Level of consciousness: RASS or orientation to person/place/time; emergence from anesthesia progressing normally?
  • Pain: Assess using 0–10 NRS or behavioral scale if not yet verbal; anticipate pain from incision
  • Surgical site: Dressing intact and dry? Drains patent and output documented? Any excessive bleeding through dressing?
  • Temperature: Hypothermia common post-op (core temp <36°C); apply warm blankets or forced-air warming device
  • Urine output: Foley catheter output or first void after catheter removal

DVT Prevention

  • Sequential compression devices (SCDs) on lower extremities BEFORE induction; remove only when patient is ambulatory
  • Early ambulation: first ambulation within 8–24 hr post-op if clinically stable (single most effective intervention)
  • Pharmacologic prophylaxis: enoxaparin (Lovenox) or heparin SC ordered per protocol; usually starts 12–24 hr post-op
  • Hydration: IV fluids to prevent hemoconcentration
  • Assess for signs of DVT with every VS: calf pain, tenderness, swelling, redness, warmth; Homans sign (calf pain with dorsiflexion) is NOT a reliable test
  • If DVT suspected: elevate extremity; notify provider; Doppler ultrasound ordered; therapeutic anticoagulation started

Post-Operative Pain Management

  • Multimodal approach: scheduled acetaminophen + NSAIDs + regional analgesia + opioids PRN breakthrough (ERAS protocol)
  • Assess pain score BEFORE and 30–60 min AFTER each intervention; document both
  • Opioids in PACU: give in small IV doses, titrate to response; monitor SpO2, RR, and sedation closely
  • PCA (Patient-Controlled Analgesia): patient presses button for small opioid doses; educate patient and family (NEVER let family press the button — "PCA by proxy" causes oversedation)
  • Epidural analgesia: assess dermatomal level of block; monitor for hypotension (most common side effect); respiratory depression (high block); motor block; epidural hematoma (back pain + motor deficit = EMERGENCY)
  • Pillow splinting: teach patient to hold pillow firmly against incision during coughing or movement

PACU Discharge Criteria

  • Modified Aldrete score ≥9 (or facility-specific criteria)
  • Stable vital signs for at least 30–60 min
  • Pain controlled (NRS ≤4 at rest)
  • Nausea/vomiting controlled
  • Surgical site stable (no active hemorrhage)
  • Consciousness and orientation appropriate
  • SpO2 maintained on room air or same level of O2 as pre-operatively
  • Temperature ≥36°C
  • Voided (or catheter in place) for outpatient procedures

NCLEX High-Yield Points

  • First post-op fever (day 1–2): Wind = atelectasis; treat with IS, deep breathing, ambulation
  • 5 W's of post-op fever: Wind (atelectasis), Water (UTI), Wound, Walking (DVT/PE), Wonder drugs
  • Aldrete score ≥9 = safe PACU discharge; checks activity, respiration, BP, consciousness, SpO2
  • Post-op urinary retention: assess if no void 6–8 hr; bladder scan; in-and-out catheterization
  • Evisceration: cover with MOIST sterile gauze; do NOT push organs back; NPO; call surgeon STAT
  • PCA: patient presses button only; NEVER family — "PCA by proxy" causes opioid overdose
  • Epidural hematoma: back pain + new motor deficit = emergency; MRI immediately
  • Incentive spirometry: q1h while awake post-op; prevents atelectasis (primary cause of post-op fever days 1–2)
  • Post-op hypothermia (<36°C): warm blankets, forced-air warming; causes shivering, coagulopathy, increased O2 demand
