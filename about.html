We built the financial resource we wish existed for nurses.

Real numbers. Real trade-offs. No advice that could apply to anyone.

“Our mission is simple: help nurses stop leaving money on the table. You completed a demanding degree, you work brutal shifts, and you deserve financial clarity — not generic blog posts that could apply to any profession.”

Why This Resource Exists

Nurses earn well. A new graduate in a high-cost city can earn $75,000–$90,000 in their first year. A seasoned ICU travel nurse doing back-to-back 13-week contracts can clear $120,000 or more. And yet, the financial advice available to nurses is either too generic (“max out your retirement account!”) or too surface-level to be useful.

Nobody was doing the actual math: what does PSLF really save you over 10 years versus refinancing? What happens to your tax-free travel stipends if you don’t maintain a valid tax home? How do you negotiate a contract extension without leaving thousands on the table?

Nurse Financial Freedom was created by a team of healthcare and finance professionals dedicated to answering exactly these questions — with the specificity that nursing professionals deserve.

What You’ll Find Here

Every topic on this site connects back to one question: what does this mean for a nurse’s actual paycheck, career, and retirement? We cover:

Student Loans & PSLF IDR plans, forgiveness timelines, refinancing trade-offs, and qualifying employer traps.
Travel Nursing Pay How contracts work, stipend tax rules, tax home requirements, and agency red flags.
Retirement & 403(b) 403(b) vs. 457(b) comparisons, pension vesting, and Roth vs. traditional decisions on a nurse’s income.
Side Income & Career Ladders CRNA pathways, NP vs. CNS salary comparisons, legal nurse consulting, and remote clinical roles.
Budgeting & Debt The 50/30/20 rule applied to nursing paychecks, shift differential planning, and pay period budgeting.
Investing Fundamentals Index funds, Roth IRAs, HSAs, and building a portfolio that survives per-diem gaps and contract gaps.
