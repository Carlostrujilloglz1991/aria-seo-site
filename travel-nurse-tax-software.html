Bottom line: Standard consumer tax software (TurboTax, H&R Block, FreeTaxUSA) handles travel nurse taxes, but none of them are optimized for the complexity you face — multi-state returns, tax-free stipend qualification, duplicate city/county tax issues, and the nuances of maintaining a tax home. Travel nurse taxes are genuinely more complicated than standard employee taxes, and the consequences of getting them wrong run in both directions: overpay through poor deduction tracking and you leave thousands on the table; misqualify tax-free stipends and you face IRS scrutiny and potential back taxes plus penalties. Understanding the basics — and choosing the right tool for your situation — is worth a few hours of your time before each filing season.

The Core Travel Nurse Tax Issues

Tax Home and Per Diem Qualification

The IRS allows travel nurses to receive housing and meal stipends tax-free if they are "temporarily away from home" for work. The key requirement: you must have a legitimate tax home — a permanent place of residence where you incur ongoing costs (rent or mortgage, utilities, maintaining the property) even while on assignment. Nurses who don't maintain a real tax home lose the right to tax-free stipends. This is the most commonly misunderstood and most consequential issue in travel nurse taxation.

Multi-State Filing

Every state where you earn income — including wages earned on assignment — may require you to file a state income tax return. In a year with three 13-week assignments across three different states, you may owe state income tax in three states plus your home state. Each state handles nonresident taxation differently. Standard consumer tax software can handle this, but it adds cost (most charge per state return) and complexity.

Deduction Tracking

Travel nurses can deduct legitimate business expenses — licensing fees, professional association dues, continuing education, work-specific clothing and supplies, and in some situations, unreimbursed travel expenses. Tracking these throughout the year rather than reconstructing them at tax time is significantly easier and more accurate.

Tax Software Options for Travel Nurses

Keeper Tax: Best Purpose-Built Option

Keeper is the tax software that has built the most credibility in travel nursing communities in 2025-2026. It connects to bank and credit card accounts to automatically identify and categorize potential deductions throughout the year — a significant advantage over year-end reconstruction. The AI-powered deduction identification knows the categories relevant to travel nurses and flags transactions that likely qualify.

Keeper's travel nurse-specific features include per diem tracking, multiple state return handling, and guidance on tax home documentation. The year-end filing workflow handles multi-state returns within the same platform. At approximately $192/year ($16/month), Keeper pays for itself if it identifies even one deduction category you would have missed.

TurboTax (Self-Employed or Premium Tier)

TurboTax is the most widely used tax software in the U.S. and handles travel nurse complexity adequately if you use the right tier. The Self-Employed tier ($130-$170 for federal + state) includes guidance on business deductions and handles multi-state filing. The interview-based approach walks you through each situation without requiring tax expertise. The limitation: TurboTax doesn't know that you're a travel nurse. It won't prompt you for travel-nurse-specific deductions or flag tax home issues. You need to know what to input.

H&R Block (Premium or Self-Employed)

H&R Block is functionally comparable to TurboTax for travel nurse use cases. The Self-Employed tier handles business deductions and multi-state returns. H&R Block's advantage: they have physical office locations if you want to review a complicated return with a human before filing, at no additional cost with certain service tiers. The same limitation as TurboTax: you need to know what deductions to claim — the software won't prompt you for travel-nurse-specific items.

FreeTaxUSA

FreeTaxUSA is the best option for travel nurses with relatively straightforward situations — one or two states, clear tax home, no unusual deduction categories. Federal filing is free; state returns are $14.99 each. The interface is less polished than TurboTax but fully functional. For a travel nurse doing a two-state return with standard deductions and well-documented per diem, FreeTaxUSA saves $100-$150 over TurboTax with equivalent accuracy.

Travel Nurse CPA: When to Pay for a Professional

A travel nurse CPA is worth the cost ($400-$800 for a full-service annual filing) in several situations: your first year of travel nursing, when you work in four or more states in a year, if you've had any IRS correspondence about your stipends, if you're uncertain about your tax home qualification, or if your total income was $150,000+ (the deduction complexity increases proportionally with income).

Finding a CPA who specializes in travel nursing matters more than finding a low price. A general CPA who doesn't know the IRS treatment of tax-free nursing stipends may give you incorrect advice or miss deductions that a specialist would capture. Ask specifically: "Have you prepared returns for travel nurses before? How do you handle the tax home qualification analysis?" Travel nursing CPA communities on Reddit and Facebook groups can recommend specialists with verified track records.

Year-round tracking tip: Open a dedicated bank account for all work-related deductible expenses. Licensing fees, CE courses, professional dues, work-specific equipment — run these through the dedicated account. At year-end, the statement is your deduction record. This single habit reduces tax prep time by hours and improves deduction accuracy significantly.

Tax Home: The Most Important Document You're Probably Not Keeping

Your ability to receive tax-free stipends depends entirely on the IRS finding that you have a legitimate tax home. Document this every year: keep records of rent or mortgage payments at your permanent residence, utility bills, any mail received there, evidence of continued ties to the community (voter registration, vehicle registration, bank account address). If you're audited — and travel nurses are a higher-audit-risk category than average — these records are your defense.
