Nurse Salary by State in 2026: The Complete Breakdown

Part of the Nurse Money Hub — salaries, side income, and nurse FIRE in one map.

The national median RN salary in 2026 sits around $88,900 — but that number hides a $70,000 spread between the lowest- and highest-paying states. Where you work is the single biggest variable in your lifetime nursing income, often worth more than a specialty credential or an extra shift every week. Here is the full breakdown.

The gap in one sentence: A staff RN in California earns a median $133,000. The same RN doing the same job in Alabama earns $62,000. That $71,000 annual difference, compounded over a 30-year career, is worth more than $4 million in forgone earnings and investment growth.

What actually drives the gap

Three factors do most of the work. First, cost of living — but state salary differences outpace COL differences more often than people expect. California's COL is roughly 35–40% above the national average; its RN salary premium is over 50%, meaning nurses in California are genuinely better off in real purchasing power, not just nominally. Second, union density — states where nursing unions are strong consistently pay $15,000–$25,000 more than comparable non-union states at the same hospital size. Third, mandatory nurse-to-patient ratios — California's ratio law creates structural demand for more RNs per shift, which bids up wages.

Specialty adds more on top

State median salaries are for all RNs. ICU, CRNA, and OR nurses earn substantially more. An ICU nurse in California can realistically hit $155,000–$175,000 with overtime and differentials. Cost of living-adjusted rankings

When you adjust for COL, the ranking shifts. Texas ($78,400 median) and Florida ($72,800) move up because their COL is 15–20% below California's but their salaries are only 40–45% lower. Washington state holds its position well — its COL is high but not California-extreme, and its $106,500 median translates to genuinely strong purchasing power. Alaska's $104,800 looks worse on a COL-adjusted basis because Anchorage costs rival Seattle.
