Clipboard Health is a shift-marketplace app that lets nurses and CNAs book individual per-diem shifts at nearby facilities — usually next-day or same-week — without going through a traditional staffing agency. Here's how it actually works and who it fits.

The one-line version: It's the "gig economy" model applied to nursing. You see open shifts on a map, book the ones you want, work them, and get paid quickly — often with instant-pay options. The trade-off is that you're managing your own schedule, taxes, and reliability score.

How the marketplace works

After you upload your license and required documents (and clear the credentialing checks), the app shows open shifts at post-acute and long-term-care facilities in your area — skilled nursing facilities, assisted living, and some acute settings depending on the market. Each shift lists the pay rate, hours, and facility rating up front. You book what fits your life, show up, clock in through the app, and get paid. Many markets offer same-day or next-day cash-out for a small fee, which is the feature nurses talk about most.

Pay: what to actually expect

Rates vary heavily by region, credential (RN vs LPN/LVN vs CNA), shift type, and how desperate the facility is to fill the slot. Hard-to-fill overnight and weekend shifts, and last-minute openings, pay noticeably more than a routine weekday day shift. Because you see the rate before booking, there's no negotiation — you either take the posted rate or you don't. That transparency is a genuine advantage over some agency arrangements where the bill rate is hidden.

Read the classification carefully. Clipboard Health has operated with both W-2 and 1099 arrangements depending on the market and over time. If you're paid as a 1099 independent contractor, no taxes are withheld — you're responsible for self-employment tax and quarterly estimates, and you can deduct mileage and supplies. If W-2, taxes come out but you lose most of those deductions. Confirm which applies to you before you rely on the take-home number.

The attendance and cancellation rules

The marketplace only works if facilities can trust that booked shifts get worked. So the platform enforces attendance policies: canceling too close to a shift, or no-showing, can trigger penalties, account restrictions, or fees, and it damages your reliability standing. Some markets have charged cancellation fees to workers, which has been a point of controversy. Before you book, know the specific cancellation window and penalty structure in your market — treat a booked shift like a real commitment.

Who it fits — and who it doesn't

It fits nurses who want to pick up extra shifts around a staff job, parents who need to build their own schedule, and anyone testing several facilities before committing. It fits less well if you want predictable weekly income, benefits, or the mentorship of a consistent unit. Post-acute and SNF environments also demand independence — you're often the license on the floor with less onboarding, so it suits experienced nurses more than brand-new grads.

Bottom line: Clipboard Health is a legitimate way to turn open per-diem shifts into flexible income with fast pay. The catches are self-managed taxes (especially on 1099), strict cancellation rules, and thin-to-no benefits. Use it as a supplement or a schedule-your-own primary — and always read the market-specific pay classification and attendance policy before booking.
