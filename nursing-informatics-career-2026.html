Nursing Informatics Career Guide 2026

Nursing informatics is one of the fastest-growing nursing specialties — and one of the few paths that significantly reduces physical demand while increasing pay and schedule flexibility.

What Does a Nursing Informatics Specialist Do?

Nursing informatics integrates nursing science with data management and information technology to improve patient care. Core responsibilities include: EHR implementation and optimization (Epic, Cerner, Meditech), clinical decision support rule-building, workflow analysis and redesign, staff education on technology systems, data analytics for quality improvement, and bridging clinical teams with IT departments.

How to Break In From Bedside

The fastest path into informatics: become your unit's "super user" during an EHR implementation. Super users receive extra training, build relationships with the informatics team, and are often recruited directly into informatics roles after go-live. If your hospital isn't currently implementing, volunteer for your EHR optimization committee.

Epic certification path: Epic offers certifications in specific modules (Orders, Inpatient Clinical, etc.). You cannot take these directly — they're employer-sponsored. Getting an informatics role that provides Epic training access is the first step. Many new informatics nurses start in hospital-employed roles specifically to gain Epic credentials.

Credentials for Nursing Informatics

  • RN-BC (Informatics): ANCC board certification in nursing informatics — requires BSN, 2 years RN experience, 30 hours informatics CEU, and passing exam
  • CPHIMS: Certified Professional in Health Informatics and Information Management (HIMSS) — not nursing-specific but widely recognized
  • MSN Nursing Informatics: Online programs at WGU, University of Phoenix, and multiple state universities — typically 18–24 months

Remote Informatics Roles

Nursing informatics is one of the few clinical-nursing-adjacent careers with abundant remote opportunities. EHR optimization, clinical decision support rule-building, and data analytics can all be done remotely. Epic implementations typically require travel, but ongoing optimization roles are frequently fully remote.
