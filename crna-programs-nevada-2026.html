CRNA Programs in Nevada

Part of the CRNA Career Hub — browse every related guide in one place.

Nevada went from zero nurse anesthesia programs to two in just a few years. Roseman University in Henderson launched the state's first CRNA doctorate, and UNLV's School of Nursing has since added its own accredited track in Las Vegas — giving in-state nurses real, growing options.

The landscape: Nevada now has two COA-recognized nurse anesthesia programs — Roseman University in Henderson (the state's first) and the University of Nevada, Las Vegas (UNLV), which gained accreditation in 2025 and enrolled its first cohort. Both are new, so confirm current details before applying.

Roseman University's three-year, hybrid-online DNPNA program in Henderson was the first nurse anesthesia doctorate offered in Nevada, created to meet the state's growing demand for anesthesia providers. It is approved by the Council on Accreditation of Nurse Anesthesia Educational Programs (COA) and prepares graduates with the clinical training required to sit for the national certification exam.

UNLV's School of Nursing added a CRNA track to its DNP program, with accreditation effective May 2025 and a first cohort now enrolled. As a public university option in Las Vegas, it broadens access for Nevada nurses who want to train in-state. Because the program is brand new, cohort size, tuition, and admission specifics are still settling — verify directly with the school.

Admission Requirements

Both Nevada programs follow the national CRNA template: a BSN (or higher) with a competitive GPA, a current unencumbered RN license, and a minimum of one year of full-time critical-care experience. As everywhere, the ICU requirement is non-negotiable, and competitive applicants typically bring more than the minimum year, high-acuity experience, and the CCRN credential — see the CCRN guide. For the full checklist, review the general CRNA school requirements.

Two new in-state options is a real win. Nevada applicants can now apply to both Roseman and UNLV without leaving the state. With both programs young, applying to both — and adding nearby states — maximizes your odds. Arizona, California, and Utah are all within reach.
Confirm the current numbers. Both programs are recent, so cohort size, tuition, credit count, and admission rules are still settling and can change year to year. Treat the figures here as a planning baseline and verify each program's current details on its official site and the COA accredited-programs list before applying.

Application Strategy for Nevada

Apply to both Roseman and UNLV — there's no reason to choose only one when both are in-state — and build a regional list that includes Arizona, California, and Utah to give yourself realistic odds across a competitive applicant pool. Strengthen your file with the required ICU year (ideally more), the CCRN, and a sharp personal statement. For realistic expectations, see CRNA acceptance rates.

Bottom Line

Nevada now offers two accredited paths to CRNA: Roseman University's DNPNA in Henderson, the state's first, and UNLV's newly accredited CRNA track in Las Vegas. Both are young programs, so apply to both and round out your list regionally. For the bigger picture, see how to become a CRNA and the CRNA job outlook.
